Hitachi Energy India — NSE: POWERINDIA · MD & CEO: Venu Nuguri · Hitachi ABB Power Grid
Business Overview

Hitachi Energy India (formerly ABB Power Grid) is India's leading power technology company in Transmission & Distribution (T&D). Carved out when ABB divested its Power Grid division to Hitachi in 2020, it provides the full grid-to-grid value chain — from ultra-high voltage transformers and HVDC systems to grid automation and digital solutions.

Products: Power transformers (HVDC, EHV 765kV), switchgear (GIS/AIS), grid automation, protection & control, FACTS, EV charging, power quality systems.

Key Markets FY26: Data Centres (largest Q4 segment — AI infra), Rail & Metro, Exports (36.8% of Q4 orders — US/Europe/APAC), Renewables, Utilities.

Capex: ₹2,000 Cr greenfield large power transformer plant in Karjan, Vadodara approved Q4 FY26 — on top of ₹2,000 Cr Oct 2024 announcement. Total cumulative capex commitment: ₹4,000 Cr — largest in Indian electrical equipment sector.

ABB India — NSE: ABB · Jan-Dec FY · MD: Sanjeev Sharma · ABB Switzerland parent
Business Overview

ABB India covers Electrification (switchgear, EV charging, solar), Motion (motors, drives), Process/Automation, and Smart Infrastructure (building electrification). Follows January-December fiscal year.

Key FY26 Event: Slump sale of Robotics & Discrete Automation business to ABB Robotics India on March 1, 2026 for ₹1,568 Cr — generating ₹1,658 Cr exceptional gain, inflating reported Q4 PAT to ₹1,784 Cr. Core continuing operations PAT fell 25%.

Post-Robotics segments: Electrification, Motion, Automation (renamed from Process Automation Jan 2026), Smart Infrastructure. Growth focus: data centre electrification, EV charging (ABB Terra range), railways automation.

Balance Sheet: Debt-free. Reserves ₹9,000+ Cr post-Robotics gain. Near-zero finance costs. Large cash pile — capital allocation decision (dividend/buyback) is the key investor question.

Bosch Limited — NSE: BOSCHLTD · Robert Bosch GmbH Germany · MD: Guruprasad Mudlapur
Business Overview

Bosch India operates in Mobility Solutions, Consumer Goods, Industrial Technology, and Energy & Building Technology. Its Power Solutions segment (EV drivetrains, battery mgmt, charging) and GCC engineering services position it as a power proxy through electrification and industrial automation.

FY26 Highlights: 2-Wheeler Power Solutions grew 63.4% in Q4 — EV transition accelerating. Mobility grew 23.3% QoQ, Power Solutions +27.4%. Consumer Goods +14.3%. Video Solutions business sold Q1 FY26 (₹556 Cr exceptional gain).

New JV: Board approved joint venture with Wheels India and Brakes India for commercial vehicle air systems — new business line.

Capital Return: Dividend FY26 ₹512/share total (₹270 final + ₹242 interim) — one of the highest in Indian industrials. Consistent dividend payer.

Siemens Energy India — NSE: SEINDIA · Demerged from Siemens Ltd 2024 · MD: Guilherme Mendonca · Oct-Sep FY
Business Overview

Siemens Energy India (NSE: SEINDIA) was demerged from Siemens Limited at 1:1 ratio in 2024 — focused purely on the energy segment. Parent: Siemens Energy AG Germany. Follows October-September FY (Jan-Mar 2026 = their Q2 FY26). Pure-play power T&D and generation company.

Segments: Power Transmission (~60% — transformers, GIS, HVDC converters) and Power Generation (~40% — turbines, generators, O&M for industrial and thermal power plants).

Capex: ₹7.4 Bn ongoing expansions + ₹20.6 Bn new greenfield transformer factory — total ₹28 Bn (≈₹2,800 Cr) transformative capex. Focus on large power transformers for 765kV and export markets (Gulf, Africa, SEA).

EBIT margin 19.4% is best in the peer group — driven by high-margin power generation services (O&M) and disciplined project execution.

Voltamp Transformers — NSE: VOLTAMP · Vadodara · CMD: Kunjal Mehta · Zero debt · Pure-play transformers
Business Overview

Voltamp Transformers is India's most disciplined pure-play power transformer maker — exclusively in distribution and power transformers (up to 220kV / 100 MVA). Selective order intake: refuses below-margin orders, maintaining premium customer base and high ROE.

Q4 FY26 Stumble: Hit by (1) negative MTM treasury losses (other income −₹10.06 Cr vs +₹26.14 Cr Q3), (2) one-time provisions, (3) CRGO steel (cold-rolled grain-oriented) cost spikes. Management confirmed these are transient; FY26 full-year business intact — record revenue ₹2,154 Cr.

Expansion: New power transformer factory under construction — commences July 2026. ₹25 Cr land acquisition near Vadodara approved. Order backlog ₹1,200 Cr (10,270 MVA) entering FY27; ₹310 Cr new orders in April 2026 alone.

Dividend: 1000% = ₹100/share FY26 — signals zero-debt financial confidence despite Q4 miss.

⚡ Power School · Fundamentals of India's Power Proxy sector
What is the "Power Proxy" Theme?

"Power Proxy" refers to companies that are not power generators but benefit enormously from India's power sector investment boom. Think of them as the picks-and-shovels of the electricity revolution — they make the transformers, switchgear, grid automation, motors, and industrial electrical equipment that goes into every substation, solar plant, data centre, and EV charger built in India.

India is investing ₹9.15 lakh crore in power grid infrastructure by 2032. Every ₹1 of generation capacity needs ~₹0.50 of T&D investment. The companies in this dashboard capture that T&D and industrial electrical equipment spend. Unlike power generators (whose earnings depend on tariffs and PLF), Power Proxy companies earn revenue from capital orders with high order-book visibility, regardless of who wins the PPA.
