CB Research — Sector Verdict FY26
GE Vernova T&D India is the standout performer — ₹14,780 Cr annual order intake (+37% YoY), record backlog of ₹21,456 Cr (+49%), EBITDA margin at 27.1%, and PAT more than doubling (+103%) to ₹1,233 Cr make it the premier India T&D play. Structural beneficiary of HVDC buildout (Adani 2.5 GW Khavda corridor), grid modernisation, and renewable integration.

LG Electronics India delivered record quarterly revenue of ₹8,054 Cr (+8.1%) in Q4 FY26, with full-year FY26 revenue at ₹24,605 Cr (+1%). EBITDA margins were compressed −230 bps in Q4 due to rupee depreciation and commodity inflation, and PAT dipped 3% to ₹1,685 Cr for the year. FY27 guidance is constructive: mid-teen revenue growth and double-digit EBITDA margins, supported by Sri City export ramp, PLI incentives, and the new BEE 5-star product portfolio.

Cera Sanitaryware showed topline resilience (+7% annual, +11.4% Q4) but PAT contracted 17.2% as brass cost inflation eroded margins. The business is debt-free with ₹853 Cr cash — a structural safety net. Faucetware segment momentum (+24.3%) is a bright spot.

PG Electroplast faces a challenging year — FY26 PAT dropped 33.5% due to weak RAC demand, LPG/truck supply disruptions, and forex losses. Revenue grew only 8.6% vs its 5-year CAGR of ~56%. FY27 recovery expected with margin improvement toward 8% EBITDA.

Blue Jet Healthcare had a highly volatile FY26 — Q1 strong (+141% PAT), then a Q2/Q3 collapse in PI-API revenues, with partial Contrast Media recovery in Q4. FY26 revenue declined 8%. The company is debt-free with ₹400 Cr cash and the Vizag greenfield expansion is a positive 3-year catalyst.
