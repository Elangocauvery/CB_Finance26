🏦
Bajaj Finance Ltd — India's Most Diversified NBFC
NSE: BAJFINANCE | Founded 1987 | Bajaj Finserv Group | AUM ₹5.10 LCr
How It Makes Money
Lends across 50+ loan products at 11–28% and borrows at ~7.41% (cost of funds). The NIM spread on ₹5.10 LCr AUM generates massive NII. Additional revenue from processing fees, insurance distribution, co-branded credit cards, and EMI network fees (3.6 Cr+ EMI cards outstanding).
Revenue Pillars
Consumer Lending (55%): Consumer durables, personal loans, digital loans, EMI cards.
SME/MSME (25%): Business loans, machinery finance, working capital.
Commercial (12%): Corporate/institutional lending.
Rural B2C (8%): Rural EMI, agri-adjacent products.
Bajaj Housing Finance: Home loans, LAP, LRD — AUM ₹1.41 LCr.
Competitive Moat
Deep distribution (4,098+ locations, 242K+ points), India's largest retail EMI ecosystem, cross-sell flywheel (75.5 Mn franchise), FINAI AI platform for risk-based pricing, AAA-rated balance sheet = cheapest borrowing among NBFCs.
⚡ Unique Selling Proposition (USP)
India's only NBFC with a lending super-app — 11.93 Cr customers, 50+ loan products, 1.29 Cr new loans every quarter. The EMI card is India's de facto middle-class consumer credit card. No competitor has replicated this in 35 years of building.
Special Traits — Q4 FY26
✅ First NBFC to cross ₹5 LCr AUM milestone
✅ Credit cost improved 52 bps YoY to 1.65% (official IP)
✅ ROE hit 20% — highest in 6 quarters
✅ FINAI AI platform showing early productivity gains
⚠️ Rajiv Bajaj stepping down — leadership transition watch
Analogy
"Bajaj Finance is the Reliance Industries of Indian lending — everything under one roof, built for scale, impossible to replicate."

Shriram Finance Ltd — India's Used Vehicle Lender
NSE: SHRIRAMFIN | Founded 1974 | Chennai HQ | AUM ₹3.02 LCr | MUFG 20% stake
How It Makes Money
Lends to buyers of used commercial vehicles, passenger vehicles, and two-wheelers at 13–22%. Borrows at ~9%. Despite elevated GNPA (4.58%), actual credit losses are contained because the company deeply understands its customer segment, has strong field collection networks, and prices risk into yield. Spread model on ₹3.02 LCr AUM.
Revenue Pillars
CV (40%): Used trucks, buses, LCVs — +19.5% YoY.
PV (25%): Used cars — +19% YoY.
Two-Wheelers (10%): Expanding rapidly.
MSME + Gold + Home (25%): Rapid diversification beyond vehicles.
Insurance Broking: High-margin fee income via Shriram One app.
Competitive Moat
50 years of rural/semi-urban customer relationships, 76,000 employees, proprietary used-vehicle valuation expertise. Only AAA-rated NBFC lending profitably to the segment banks avoid. MUFG global capital + local depth = unique combination.
⚡ Unique Selling Proposition (USP)
Only AAA-rated NBFC profitably lending to India's trucking community — the backbone of India's ₹200+ trillion goods transportation sector. Shriram is the financial infrastructure for India's used vehicle economy. No global or domestic bank can replicate this 50-year network.
Special Traits — Q4 FY26
✅ MUFG (Japan's largest bank) acquires 20% stake — ₹39,618 Cr valuation
✅ AAA rating sweep — ICRA, CRISIL, India Ratings, Fitch all upgraded
✅ AUM crossed ₹3 LCr milestone for first time
✅ PAT beat — ₹3,014 Cr vs Street estimate of ₹2,600 Cr (+16%)
✅ RBI in-principle approval for Primary Dealer licence
✅ ROA 3.63% — best among diversified NBFCs
✅ Credit cost improved 70 bps YoY to ~1.7%

"Shriram Finance is the SBI of the trucking community — trusted, deeply embedded, irreplaceable in India's rural transport economy."

Cholamandalam Investment & Finance — The Murugappa NBFC
NSE: CHOLAFIN | Founded 1978 | Murugappa Group | Chennai HQ | AUM ₹2.43 LCr
How It Makes Money
Earns by financing vehicle purchases then cross-selling into home loans, LAP, SME loans, and consumer products. Blended lending yield ~15%, cost of funds ~8.5%, delivering NIM ~7.9%. Vehicle Finance is the anchor; LAP and Home Loans provide stable, secured, lower-risk revenue. Insurance income via CMSGICL adds diversification.
Revenue Pillars
Vehicle Finance (49%): Auto AUM ₹1.20 LCr, +18% YoY.
Loan Against Property (24%): +26% YoY — property-backed, lower risk.
Home Loans: +23% YoY — mortgage expansion.
SME Loans: +41% YoY — fastest growing, high-yield.
Gold Loans (New Launch): ₹1,130 Cr disbursed in first full quarter Q4 FY26.
Competitive Moat
Murugappa Group parentage — India's most respected conglomerate. AA+ credit rating. Strong dealer relationships across 4,200+ touchpoints. Group reputation opens doors banks and standalone NBFCs cannot access.
⚡ Unique Selling Proposition (USP)
The rare NBFC that built a full-spectrum lending platform starting from vehicle finance. Murugappa brand = institutional trust that no startup or foreign NBFC can buy. Q4 ROA 4.1% with ROE 22.95% on a fully diversified book is remarkable. Highest disbursement growth at 25% YoY in Q4 FY26.
Special Traits — Q4 FY26
✅ SME loans +41% YoY — high-yield emerging engine
✅ Gold Loan launched — ₹1,130 Cr disbursed in first full quarter
✅ NIM improved +40 bps YoY — CoF declining faster than yield
✅ Disbursements ₹32,913 Cr — highest ever Q4 in company history
✅ CSEL stabilising after fintech partnership exits
⚠️ ₹200 Cr precautionary management overlay for global uncertainty
Analogy

🔬 Why can Muthoot Finance NOT be compared with the other three NBFCs on standard metrics?
🔬 Muthoot Finance-ஐ மற்ற NBFCs-உடன் direct-ஆக ஒப்பிட ஏன் முடியாது?
+
Muthoot operates a fundamentally different business model — comparing its GNPA, NIM or P/B with Bajaj or Shriram is analytically misleading.

GNPA comparison invalid: Muthoot's 2.35% GNPA vs Bajaj's 1.01% looks worse. But Muthoot's NPA customers have physical gold in custody. When they default, the gold is auctioned at market price — actual credit loss is near-zero. Muthoot's GNPA measures repayment delay, not loss risk. You cannot equate a gold-secured NPA with an unsecured consumer loan NPA.

NIM comparison distorts: Muthoot NIM ~27% vs Bajaj ~10.2%. Gold loans charge 18–27% because they're short-tenor (3–12 months), high-touch, collateral-intensive. The yield compensates for custody infrastructure, auction costs, branch density. Bajaj achieving 10.2% NIM on ₹5 LCr at scale is a far harder operating feat.

PCR comparison misleads: Muthoot's PCR of ~14% looks dangerously low vs Bajaj's 60%. But with physical gold as collateral, even 5% provision is adequate — the Loss Given Default is structurally near-zero.

Correct peer group: Muthoot should be evaluated against Manappuram Gold Finance — not Bajaj, Shriram, or Cholamandalam. The right metrics are: gold AUM growth, gold price trajectory, branch productivity per kg of gold, operating leverage, and subsidiary diversification progress.

1. Consumer Durables as the Trojan Horse (1987–2010): Banks ignored TV/fridge EMI financing as too small. Bajaj Finance partnered with electronics retailers nationwide. The formula: Win customer at purchase → cross-sell for life → price risk precisely → fund cheaply → compound at scale → never blow up the balance sheet.

🏛️ What is RBI's regulatory policy on Upper Layer NBFCs — and how does it affect these four companies differently?
🏛️ RBI Upper Layer NBFC கொள்கை என்ன — நான்கு நிறுவனங்களை எப்படி வித்தியாசமாக பாதிக்கிறது?
+
RBI's Scale-Based Regulation (SBR) 2021 is the most significant structural change in NBFC regulation in a decade. All four companies are classified as Upper Layer (UL) NBFCs — the most regulated tier.

Core Upper Layer requirements:
• CRAR minimum 15% (all four comply comfortably: Bajaj 21.55%, Shriram 34%, Chola 19.21%, Muthoot ~24%)
• ECL (Expected Credit Loss) provisioning — same forward-looking model as banks, mandatory since March 2025
• LCR (Liquidity Coverage Ratio) — must hold high-quality liquid assets for 30-day stress scenario
• 90-day NPA recognition — no flexibility, same as banks
• Corporate governance: independent directors, board risk committee, mandatory internal audit

Company-specific impacts:
Bajaj Finance: ECL migration caused ₹147 Cr Q4 overlay. Being the largest, every regulatory change hits them first and hardest in absolute terms. But their governance infrastructure is already the strongest.

📊 What is ECL provisioning — and why did it cause one-off charges at Bajaj and Cholamandalam in Q4 FY26?
📊 ECL provisioning என்ன — Bajaj, Chola-ல் Q4-ல் one-off charge ஏன் வந்தது?
+
ECL (Expected Credit Loss) is a fundamental shift in how NBFCs provision for bad loans — from reactive to proactive.

Old model (Incurred Loss): Provision only when a loan actually turns NPA. Like insuring your car only after an accident — too late.

ECL model (RBI-mandated for Upper Layer): Provision based on the statistical probability of loss occurring in the future — even for current performing loans. Three stages:
• Stage 1 (Performing): 12-month expected loss — ~0.3–0.5% provision
• Stage 2 (Significant deterioration): Lifetime expected loss — ~3–8% provision
• Stage 3 (NPA): Lifetime expected loss — ~40–70% provision

Why one-off hits in Q4 FY26:
When Upper Layer NBFCs migrated to ECL, they had to provision for Stage 1 and Stage 2 loans that previously had zero or minimal provisions under the old model. This created a transitional "catch-up" charge. Bajaj Finance: ₹147 Cr additional overlay. Cholamandalam: ₹200 Cr (partly ECL, partly management discretionary overlay for global macro uncertainty — US tariffs, geopolitical risk).

Why this is a bullish signal, not bearish:
1. These are one-time charges — they don't repeat in future quarters.
2. ECL creates a stronger, more credible balance sheet that institutional investors and global partners like MUFG value.
3. If the macro environment stabilises, Stage 2 overlays can be written back as income in future quarters — providing PAT upside.
4. Companies that voluntarily add management overlays (Chola's ₹200 Cr) demonstrate conservative management — a quality signal.

🏦 NBFC vs Bank — key differences and which is better to invest in?
🏦 NBFC மற்றும் Bank வித்தியாசம் — எதில் invest சிறந்தது?
+
Key differences:
• Deposits: Banks accept savings/current (CASA) at low cost. NBFCs cannot — they fund via FDs, NCDs, borrowings at higher cost.
• Cost of Funds: Banks ~6–7%; NBFCs ~7.5–9.5%. Structural disadvantage for NBFCs.
• Niche Lending: NBFCs serve segments banks avoid (used vehicles, gold, microfinance) — enabling higher yields that offset higher CoF.
• Regulation: Banks: CRR, SLR, CASA norms, CBS architecture. NBFCs: fewer mandatory reserves, more operational flexibility.
• NIM: NBFCs 7–27% vs Banks 3–4.5% — NBFCs earn far higher NIM from niche lending.

Which is better to invest in? ⚠️ Not investment advice. NBFCs offer higher growth, higher ROA, and niche franchise value. Banks offer more stable funding, lower credit cost, and more predictable earnings. Bajaj Finance at 6.2x P/B vs HDFC Bank at ~2.5x P/B — the NBFC premium reflects superior ROA and franchise. Choose based on your risk appetite and time horizon.
Banks: CASA deposits வாங்குகின்றன (மலிவு). NBFCs: FDs, NCDs மூலம் expensive-ஆக fund செய்கின்றன.

NBFCs banks போகாத segments-ல் கடன் — higher yield. NIM: NBFCs 7–27% vs Banks 3–4.5%.

எதில் invest? NBFCs — higher growth, higher ROA, niche franchise. Banks — stable funding, predictable earnings. (This is analysis, not advice. Consult SEBI advisor.)

📈 What is PPOP and why does it reveal more than PAT?
📈 PPOP என்றால் என்ன — PAT-ஐ விட இது ஏன் அதிக உண்மையைக் காட்டுகிறது?
+
PPOP = Pre-Provision Operating Profit = NII + Other Income − Operating Expenses. Profit BEFORE any provisions for bad loans.

Why it matters: Management can increase or decrease provisions each quarter to smooth PAT. PPOP strips that out and shows the true operating earning power of the business.

Q4 FY26 example: Shriram Finance — PPOP grew 22.8% YoY (real business growth). PAT grew 41% because provisions fell sharply (credit cost normalisation). The 41% PAT looks impressive but 22.8% PPOP is the sustainable engine. The gap between PPOP growth and PAT growth = provisioning mechanics.

Bajaj Finance PPOP: ₹9,415 Cr (+18.2%) — the real earnings power. PAT of ₹5,553 Cr is after ₹2,008 Cr provisions and ~₹2,000 Cr tax.

Always compare PPOP growth alongside PAT growth for a complete picture of NBFC earnings quality.
PPOP = Provision போடுவதற்கு முன்னால் சம்பாதிக்கும் profit. NII + Other Income − Operating Expenses.

Management provision-ஐ மாற்றி PAT-ஐ ஒவ்வொரு quarter-லும் adjust செய்யலாம். PPOP அதை விலக்கி உண்மையான business strength-ஐ காட்டுகிறது.

⚖️ RBI gold loan regulation — what are the rules and how do they affect Muthoot?
⚖️ RBI gold loan regulation என்ன — Muthoot-ஐ எப்படி பாதிக்கிறது?
+
RBI gold loan tightening (FY25–26):
1. LTV cap at 75%: Companies lending above 75% of gold value had to reduce balances. Caused temporary slowdown in FY25 H1 — which is why FY26's 50% growth is partly a recovery.
2. Bullet repayment restriction: Gold loans above certain tenors require periodic interest servicing, not a single bullet repayment at end.
3. Auction process norms: Stricter timelines for auctioning defaulted gold pledges.
4. End-use declaration: Gold loans above ₹5 lakh need documented end-use purpose.

Impact on Muthoot: Muthoot proactively restructured its book in FY25 to comply. The FY26 50% AUM growth reflects both recovery from compliance-led slowdown and genuine gold price-driven demand surge. RBI Upper Layer NBFC status for 4 consecutive years shows regulator's confidence in Muthoot's governance.

Risk level: Moderate but manageable. New LTV or tenor norms could temporarily slow disbursements in FY27 — but Muthoot's branch network and brand ensure customers return once compliance adjustments are made.
