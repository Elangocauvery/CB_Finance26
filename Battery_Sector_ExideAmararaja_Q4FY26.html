🔋 Why Battery Stocks Are Unique

Battery companies sit at the intersection of old-economy manufacturing and new-economy energy transition. Lead-acid batteries remain essential for automobiles, telecom towers, and power backup. Simultaneously, lithium-ion is reshaping transport and grid storage globally. The best companies must execute in both worlds simultaneously.

India is in an energy storage golden decade (FY26–FY35): EV adoption + solar storage + telecom lithium migration + grid BESS + inverter/UPS penetration. Whoever wins the Li-ion transition wins the next 20 years.

⚡ Battery Technology Landscape — Types, Models & Renewable Applications
🔋
1. Lead-Acid Battery (LAB)
Mature · Dominant FY26 Revenue for Both Companies
VRLA — Valve Regulated Lead Acid
Two subtypes: AGM (Absorbent Glass Mat) and Gel.
AGM: Acid absorbed in glass fibre mat. Sealed, maintenance-free. Used in UPS, inverters, home power backup, telecom towers. Exide's PowerSafe, AMEM's Amaron PowerStack are AGM products.
Gel: Acid in silica gel. Better for deep-cycle, extreme temperatures. Used in solar hybrid systems, marine.
Zero maintenanceTelecom BTSHome UPS
Flooded / Wet Cell (Conventional)
Oldest design. Liquid electrolyte. Requires periodic water top-up. Used in automotive OEM (starter batteries for ICE vehicles), industrial forklifts, large UPS.
Market share: Both Exide and AMEM together hold 70%+ of India's automotive LAB market.
Lowest costMaintenance neededICE vehicles

LAB in Renewable Applications: Deep-cycle LAB variants handle 200–500 cycles and serve off-grid solar backup. However, LAB faces structural headwinds in solar storage:

FactorLABLi-ion (LFP)
Cycle Life200–5003,000–6,000
Energy Density30–50 Wh/kg120–160 Wh/kg
Depth of Discharge50%80–95%
Self-Discharge/month5–8%1–3%
Cost/kWh (India 2026)₹6,000–8,000₹15,000–22,000

LAB remains preferred where upfront cost dominates and cycling is infrequent — rural solar pumps, small off-grid systems. For utility-scale BESS, LAB is rapidly displaced by LFP.

⚡
2. Lithium-Ion Battery (Li-ion) — The Growth Engine
High Growth · Gigafactory Investment by Both Exide & AMEM

Li-ion is the dominant technology for EVs, grid-scale BESS, and consumer electronics. The key variable is cathode chemistry — each chemistry is a different "model" optimised for different applications. Exide's gigafactory (Bengaluru, 6 GWh scalable to 12 GWh) supports both LFP and NMC in both cylindrical and prismatic formats. AMEM's Giga Corridor initially targets NMC.

LFP — Lithium Iron Phosphate
Full name: LiFePO₄
Strengths: Safest Li-ion chemistry (no thermal runaway), longest cycle life (3,000–6,000 cycles), excellent temperature stability, lowest cost per cycle, 15+ year calendar life.
Weakness: Lower energy density (~120–160 Wh/kg) vs NMC.
Applications: Grid BESS, solar storage, e-rickshaws, telecom backup, buses.
India: PM Surya Ghar storage, NTPC/SECI BESS tenders. Exide cylindrical line includes LFP.
Safest chemistrySolar BESS6,000+ cycles
NMC — Nickel Manganese Cobalt Oxide
Full name: LiNiMnCoO₂ (variants: NMC 811, NMC 622, NMC 532)
Strengths: Highest energy density (200–250 Wh/kg). Best power. Longest EV range.
Weakness: Contains cobalt (expensive, Congo supply risk). ~2,000 cycles. Higher thermal risk.
Applications: Passenger EVs (4-wheelers), premium portables.
India: Tata Nexon, MG ZS, Ola S1 use NMC/similar. AMEM Phase 1 Giga Corridor = 1 GWh NMC.
Highest rangeCobalt costPremium EVs
NCA — Nickel Cobalt Aluminium Oxide
Full name: LiNiCoAlO₂
Strengths: Very high energy density (~200–260 Wh/kg). Tesla's original cylindrical cell chemistry.
Weakness: Less thermally stable than NMC. Needs sophisticated BMS. More expensive.
Applications: Tesla 3/Y, some premium e-bikes.
India relevance: Limited — not a focus for Indian gigafactories. Mentioned for completeness.
Tesla chemistryThermal risk
Cylindrical Cells (18650, 21700, 4680)
Form factor, not chemistry. Number = diameter × height in mm. 18650 = 18mm × 65mm. 4680 = 46mm × 80mm (Tesla's structural cell).
Why it matters: Cylindrical cells are the workhorse for 2-wheeler and 3-wheeler EV packs in India. Exide's Bengaluru gigafactory prioritises cylindrical lines for 2W/3W EVs — customer sample deliveries expected Q1 FY27.
2W/3W EV packsHigh volumeStandardised format
Prismatic & Pouch Cells
Prismatic: Rectangular hard case. Better space efficiency, easier thermal management. Used in 4W EVs and stationary BESS. Exide's prismatic line (LFP/NMC) targets 3W EV + stationary — trials June–July 2026.
Pouch: Flexible laminated casing. Highest energy density by volume. Used in laptops, mobile phones, some EVs.
4W EVsGrid BESSPouch for mobile
🔬
3. Advanced & Emerging Battery Chemistries
Emerging · Not commercialised at India scale as of FY26
Solid-State Battery (SSB)
Replaces liquid electrolyte with solid material (ceramic, polymer, sulphide). Theoretical energy density 2–3× Li-ion. No flammability. Cycle life 10,000+.
India timeline: Global commercialisation 2028–2032 (Toyota, QuantumScape). India manufacturing before 2030 unlikely. Long-term disruption risk to current gigafactory investments.
Post-2030Disruption risk
Sodium-Ion Battery (Na-ion)
Uses sodium instead of lithium. Sodium is 1,000× more abundant. Energy density ~100–160 Wh/kg (below LFP).
India relevance: CATL commercialising in China for entry EVs. Relevant for low-cost 2W EVs in India. Telangana government MoU for Na-ion manufacturing. Exide and AMEM not yet committed.
No lithium neededLow costLower density
Flow Battery (Vanadium Redox)
Energy stored in liquid electrolyte tanks. Capacity scales by tank size — ideal for 4–12 hour grid storage. Cycle life effectively unlimited.
India relevance: NTPC, SECI pilot projects for long-duration storage. Not a revenue line for Exide or AMEM — relevant as a grid storage competitor to LFP-BESS.
Grid scale 4-12hrUnlimited cyclesHigh upfront cost

Exide Industries
🏦 HDFC Life Stake — Hidden Balance Sheet Asset
Exide previously operated Exide Life Insurance Company as a subsidiary. In FY22, Exide sold Exide Life Insurance to HDFC Life Insurance — the deal was structured as a share-swap (Exide received HDFC Life equity shares, not cash). This is how Exide came to hold a significant stake in HDFC Life (~₹11,000 Cr value). FY26 mark-to-market decline ~₹850 Cr was routed through OCI (no P&L impact). No shares sold. Provides capital optionality — strategic sale would unlock cash for Li-ion without any debt. 🔵 Exide Industries — CB Verdict FY26
Exide enters FY27 from a position of financial strength rarely seen in Indian manufacturing: zero debt, highest-ever quarterly revenue, expanding domestic market share, and a gigafactory on the cusp of first customer samples. The "One-Exide" operating model is working — 92% of the business grew 16% YoY in Q4 while only telecom and exports weighed on blended growth.

The key investment question is not whether Exide can sustain its legacy LAB business — it can, comfortably. The question is whether ₹4,802 Cr invested in EESL generates competitive Li-ion cell returns. This depends on: (a) whether cylindrical cell quality meets OEM standards for 2W EVs from Q1 FY27, (b) whether India's domestic Li-ion demand scales fast enough before Chinese cells dominate on price, and (c) whether Exide can reach 6 GWh utilisation without a long cash-burn phase. The HDFC Life stake provides powerful capital optionality — strategic sale could double-fund the gigafactory with no debt.

CB Research Assessment: Quality business, clear execution track record, meaningful Li-ion optionality. Near-term margin risk from sulfur prices and export headwinds is real. FY27 will be a defining year for the Li-ion narrative.
🟢 Amara Raja E&M — CB Verdict FY26
AMEM FY26 tells two stories simultaneously: a topline that grew 9%, and a bottomline that shrank 5.2%. The margin compression is the dominant narrative — 241 basis points eroded over 6 quarters is not a blip, it is a trend. The causes are identifiable (raw material volatility, new energy investment drag, export disruption) and partially temporary, but the market is right to demand margin recovery evidence before re-rating.

The Amaron brand remains AMEM's most durable asset — and the aftermarket replacement cycle (6-7% 4W, 10-11% 2W) is a near-guaranteed revenue floor. The New Energy Business at 60% growth is the strategic hope — but it currently contributes to margin erosion, not margin expansion, until scale is reached. The Giga Corridor ambition (₹9,500 Cr) is either the boldest bet in Indian battery history or a capex trap — that question will be answered between FY27 and FY29.

CB Research Assessment: At 17x PE, the market is pricing in margin risk. If FY27 EBITDA recovers toward 14%, the earnings upgrade + multiple expansion could generate significant returns. If margin remains stuck at 11%, the valuation discount is justified. Margin trajectory is the single most important variable to watch.
