🧪 What Makes Speciality Chemicals Stocks Unique

Unlike commodity chemicals, speciality chemicals are sold on performance, purity and regulatory approval — not on price per tonne alone. A pharma intermediate that fails a customer's audit is worthless; one that passes can command a multi-year, sole-source contract. This is why the same molecule chemistry (amines, fluorochemicals, surfactants) splits into wildly different margin profiles depending on end-customer and purity grade.

India's speciality chemicals sector rides a structural China+1 sourcing-diversification wave: global pharma, agro and electronics customers are deliberately building non-China supply chains, and Indian players with regulatory track records (US FDA, EU REACH) are the natural beneficiaries — though Chinese capacity dumping remains a constant pricing-power threat in lower-differentiation segments (BOPP films, performance surfactants, commodity amines).

🔑 Why EBITDA % Comparisons Are Misleading

Acutaas Chemicals' 35.9% FY26 EBITDA margin and Galaxy Surfactants' 9.4% are not measuring the same thing — Acutaas's CDMO/specialty mix is high-IP, low-volume chemistry; Galaxy's performance surfactants are high-volume, largely pass-through commodity chemistry. Compare margin trajectory and segment mix, not absolute levels, across these business models.

⚠️ Flat Years Aren't Always a Red Flag

Both Alkyl Amines (-2.3% revenue) and Vinati Organics (-0.9% revenue) posted FY26 declines — yet both expanded margins and stayed debt-free. A flat-revenue year with rising margins and a clean balance sheet is a very different story from a flat-revenue year with rising debt (see Aarti Industries, where revenue rose but leverage also rose).

🌐 SRF's Scale Is a Different League

SRF's ₹15,787 Cr FY26 revenue dwarfs every other company in this comparison — its Chemicals segment alone (₹7,779 Cr) is roughly equal to Aarti Industries' entire FY26 revenue. Direct multiple comparisons should adjust for this scale and conglomerate-versus-pure-play structure.

✅ The Debt-Free Club

Vinati Organics, Galaxy Surfactants, Acutaas Chemicals and (standalone) Balaji Amines and Alkyl Amines all carry effectively zero or minimal net debt. This is unusually clean for a speciality chemicals peer set and reflects a sector where most growth capex has historically been self-funded rather than debt-funded.

⚠️ Aarti Industries — Rising Leverage to Watch

Net D/E rose to 0.72× from 0.62× as borrowings increased to ₹4,921 Cr while operating cashflow fell 37% to ₹781 Cr on a receivables build-up. This is capex-funded growth (MMA/DCA expansion), not distress — but it is the one balance sheet in this peer set genuinely worth monitoring closely into FY27.

🏭 SRF — Large but Funded by Scale

SRF's ₹3,854 Cr net debt looks large in absolute terms, but at ~1.0× EBITDA against a ₹3,705 Cr FY26 EBITDA base, leverage is manageable — and it is directly funding the ₹2,300 Cr Odisha HFO plant, a structurally advantaged, regulation-driven growth project.

SRF's combination of scale (₹15,787 Cr revenue), disciplined capital allocation (deferring Indore BOPP, committing to Odisha HFO), and a genuine regulatory tailwind (global HFC phase-down) makes it the highest-quality large-cap exposure here — net debt is the only line item to track as the ₹2,300 Cr+ capex programme executes.

⚠️ Most Caution Warranted — Galaxy Surfactants

Strong revenue growth (+24.1% FY26) is being entirely offset by EBITDA margin compression (12.0%→9.4%) — a structural-looking cost/pricing mismatch rather than a one-quarter blip. The balance sheet (debt-free, net cash) provides resilience, but the investment case needs visible margin stabilisation before it re-rates.

🔍 Watch Closely — Alkyl Amines & Vinati Organics

Both posted flat-to-declining FY26 revenue, yet both expanded margins and stayed debt-free — a sign of disciplined operators navigating soft demand rather than businesses in structural decline. Vinati's new fragrance/food-additive lines and Alkyl Amines' DGTR-protected pricing are the specific catalysts to track through FY27.
