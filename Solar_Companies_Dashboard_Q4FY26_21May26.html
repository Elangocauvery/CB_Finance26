Business Model Differentiation

How each company makes money · Revenue structure · Risk profile · Investor lens
🏭 Manufacturer
Waaree Energies, Premier Energies, Fujiyama
  • Sells physical solar modules/cells to installers, EPC companies, utilities
  • Revenue = Volume (GW) × Average Selling Price (₹/W)
  • Margin risk: Chinese ASP competition, BCD/ALMM policy dependence
  • Capital: Heavy — manufacturing plants ₹5,000–10,000+ Cr capex
  • Working capital intensive; 60–90 day receivables
  • USP: Scale + ALMM compliance + backward integration
  • Investor thesis: Volume growth × margin expansion
⚡ IPP / Developer
Adani Green, ACME Solar, NTPC Green
  • Builds, owns & operates solar/wind/hybrid power plants
  • Revenue = Units generated × PPA tariff (25-yr contract)
  • Margin: EBITDA 85–93% (IPP typical) but interest eats profit
  • Capital: Very heavy — ₹4–6 Cr/MW project capex, 70:30 D:E
  • Risk: project execution delays, state discom payment risk
  • USP: PPA annuity visibility, BESS/FDRE premium tariffs
  • Investor thesis: Capacity MW × PLF × tariff − debt cost
🔧 EPC Contractor
Sterling & Wilson, Waaree Renewable Tech.
  • Builds solar projects for IPPs, utilities, C&I customers for a fee
  • Revenue = Order book execution × billing milestones
  • Margin: Thin, 3–8% net (S&W) to 18%+ (WRTL with captive modules)
  • Capital: Working capital intensive (negative WC possible)
  • Risk: commodity price spikes, liquidated damages, order delays
  • USP: Track record GW executed, O&M recurring income
  • Investor thesis: Order inflow → revenue recognition → margin improvement
🏢 C&I / B2C
CleanMax, KPI Green, Fujiyama (B2C)
  • OPEX model: Builds + owns + operates, customer pays per unit consumed
  • Revenue = Long-term energy supply agreements (ESA) + O&M fees
  • Margin: Better than EPC (~20–30% EBITDA at operating level)
  • Capital: Asset ownership but shared/subsidized by customer PPAs
  • Risk: Customer churn, credit risk of corporate off-takers
  • USP: Corporate RE target fulfillment + RPO compliance
  • Investor thesis: Contracted capacity × tariff × customer stickiness

EBITDA Margin by Business Model — Why Model Choice Determines Margin
IPP model commands 85–93% EBITDA because once the plant is built, operating cost is near-zero (sunlight is free). EPC earns only 5–8% because it bears commodity & execution risk on behalf of others. Manufacturers range 18–32% depending on cell integration depth and ALMM protection.
