AU Small Finance Bank
Jaipur, Rajasthan · NSE: AUBANK · BSE: 540611 · SFB licence: 2017 · Founded: 1996
✓ Q4 FY26
Universal Bank Licence Filed
Business: India's largest SFB by market cap (₹75,000 Cr+). Started as AU Financiers (vehicle finance NBFC), converted to SFB in 2017, merged with Fincare SFB in 2024. Full-service bank with ~2,790 touchpoints across 21 states. Loan book: Vehicle/Wheels, Micro Business Loans, Mortgages, Gold, Credit Cards, MFI (~5%). Filed for universal bank licence March 2026 — biggest re-rating catalyst in sector history for an SFB. Q4 FY26 delivered highest-ever quarterly PAT of ₹832 Cr.

Suryoday Small Finance Bank
Navi Mumbai, Maharashtra · NSE: SURYODAY · BSE: 543279 · SFB licence: 2017
✓ Q4 FY26
Turnaround in progress
Business: Smallest of the four by AUM (₹13,261 Cr). Serves unbanked/underbanked segments across 16 states with ~3.8 million customers, 712 banking outlets. Flagship product: "Vikas Loan" (individually underwritten micro-enterprise) — now 70% of IF book with disbursements up 98% YoY. Non-IF (secured: mortgages, wheels) is now 55% of advances. Promoter holds 22.4% but 22.35% is pledged — monitor carefully.

Book Value & P/B Analysis: For banks, Price-to-Book (P/B) is the primary valuation metric. A bank trading above 1x P/B implies the market expects returns above cost of equity. Premium P/B (like AU at 3.9x) reflects quality, scale, and growth optionality. Discount to P/B (Suryoday at ~0.9x) reflects asset quality stress and RoE below cost of equity. Book Value Per Share (BVPS) = Shareholders' Equity ÷ Shares Outstanding. EPS and BVPS are from latest available audited results.
