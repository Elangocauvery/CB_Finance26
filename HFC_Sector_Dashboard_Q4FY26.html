🏦 Sector Snapshot — Two Speeds, One Cycle

FY26 was a tale of two housing finance sectors. Prime/large-ticket lenders (Bajaj HFL, LIC HF, PNB Housing) navigated a falling-rate environment with NIM compression but steady, low-risk growth. Affordable/small-ticket lenders (Aadhar, India Shelter, Aavas, Home First) posted 20–33% PAT growth, riding India's structural affordable-housing wave with far higher NIMs (6–8%) but smaller absolute books.

📉 RBI Rate Cycle — The Margin Swing Factor

RBI cut the repo rate by a cumulative 125 bps through CY2025, then paused at 5.25% through H1 CY2026. This squeezed NIMs at floating-rate, large-ticket lenders (Bajaj HFL NIM 4.0%→3.8%, LIC HF NIM 2.85%→2.80% Q4 YoY) while affordable lenders with fixed/quarterly-reset structures saw margins expand (Can Fin Homes 3.82%→4.19%, Aavas +44bps QoQ to 8.45%).

🏗️ Affordable Housing — The Structural Tailwind

Affordable housing loans are 81.96% of all active home loan accounts in India (H1 FY26). HFCs' disbursement share has risen from 24.03% (FY21) to 30.12% (H1 FY26) at the cost of public-sector banks, because HFCs underwrite informal-income borrowers banks can't serve. PMAY subsidy allocation rose 133% YoY in FY26 to ₹3,500 Cr.

🔑 Why NIM Comparisons Across HFCs Are Misleading

Bajaj HFL's 3.8% NIM looks weak next to Aavas's 7.93% — but they aren't the same business. Bajaj HFL writes large-ticket, low-risk, prime salaried loans plus institutional LRD/Developer Finance at thin spreads with near-zero credit losses (GNPA 0.27%). Aavas and the affordable peers write small-ticket, higher-risk, self-employed/informal-income loans at wide spreads that compensate for higher credit cost. Comparing NIM without comparing GNPA and credit cost tells only half the story.

⚠️ The P/E Spread — Sector's Widest Valuation Dispersion

LIC HF trades at 5.2× P/E; Bajaj HFL at 28.8×. That's a 5.5× gap between the two ends of the same sector. The market is pricing growth, asset quality and ownership cleanliness — not just earnings. LIC HF's structurally slow loan growth (+4%) and legacy project-loan NPAs justify some discount, but the gap also reflects how richly Bajaj HFL's brand and parentage are already priced in.

🌸 Home First's 41% PAT Growth — Sustainable or Base-Effect?

Home First's PAT growth (+41.4%) is the fastest in this set, but provisions nearly doubled YoY (₹28.8 Cr→₹56.9 Cr) and GNPA at 1.8% is the highest among affordable peers. Some of the PAT acceleration reflects scale benefits as a smaller base compounds quickly — not necessarily a structural step-change in unit economics versus India Shelter (ROA 5.9% vs Home First's lower ROE of ~12.3%).

LIC HF's Stage 3 EAD has fallen from 2.47% to 2.16% YoY — genuine progress — but it remains structurally the worst asset-quality profile in this set, almost entirely because of the small (2.9% of book) but disproportionately troubled project/builder loan portfolio. ECL provisions fell 6.7%, which is the right direction, but investors should track Stage 3 specifically on the project book, not the blended number, to judge whether the cleanup is structural or cyclical.

🌸 Home First — Provisions Nearly Doubled. Should You Worry?

🏠 What Makes Housing Finance Stocks Unique

HFCs are spread businesses, not product businesses — they borrow money at one rate and lend it at a higher rate against a real, recoverable asset (the home). Unlike most NBFCs, the collateral (mortgaged property) is high-value, hard to abandon, and emotionally important to the borrower — this is why housing loans structurally carry the lowest credit costs of any retail lending category in India.

India's mortgage-to-GDP ratio is only ~12.2% versus ~76% in the US and ~64% in the UK — among the lowest in any large economy. This isn't a mature, saturated market; it's a multi-decade structural growth runway, further accelerated by the PMAY-U 2.0 interest subsidy and rising urbanisation.

🔵 Prime / Large-Ticket — Bajaj HFL, LIC HF, PNB Housing

📉 RBI Rate Cycle — Why NIM Direction Matters

RBI cut the repo rate by a cumulative 125bps through CY2025, then paused at 5.25% through H1 CY2026. Floating-rate liability books re-price faster than fixed-rate asset books on the way down — which is why some HFCs (Can Fin, PNB Housing) saw NIM expand even as average lending yields fell.
