SECTOR-WIDE RISKS
🔴 Common Risks — ALL Companies
🪙Copper/Aluminium Prices: LME copper spiked ~25% in Q3 FY26, compressing margins. All companies are pass-through players — price volatility inflates revenue while masking true volume growth.
⚓Middle East Export Disruption: Strait of Hormuz concerns hurt Polycab, KEI, and RR Kabel exports in Q4 FY26. Gulf region is a major export market for Indian cable makers.
🇨🇳Chinese Competition: Chinese cable imports (especially in non-US markets) continue to pressure pricing. Apar specifically flagged Chinese competition in global conductor markets.
🇺🇸US Reciprocal Tariff (26%): India-origin goods face 26% tariff in US. Severely impacts Apar (US −51% in Q3). Polycab and KEI less affected due to domestic tilt.
🏢 Company-Specific Risks
🔵Polycab: FMEG targeting 8–10% EBITDA by FY30 — still building. EPC timing volatility. ₹6,000–8,000 Cr capex commitment over 5 years creates execution risk.
🟠Havells: Lloyd fell 19% Q4 and slipped into operating loss. ₹283 Cr one-time Goldi Solar gain flatters reported PAT. Core EBITDA actually declined 4.4% YoY.
🟣RR Kabel: Inventory days 64 vs 56 in FY25 — channel stuffing risk. FMEG goodwill impairment risk flagged by auditors. Former CEO lawsuit pending.
🟢Finolex: Promoter dispute (Orbit vs Chhabria family) — prolonged governance overhang. Low growth ~10% vs sector 20–30%. Deep value but catalyst unclear.
🔷Apar: CEO Cable Solutions resigned Jan 2026. US tariff hit. Low PAT margin (~3.8–5%) amplifies any revenue swing. Transformer oil segment faces cyclical softness.

STRUCTURAL DEMAND TAILWINDS
🏗️ Domestic Drivers
✅Housing Boom: PMAY, smart cities, real estate cycle drive residential wiring demand sustainably for years.
✅Power T&D Capex: PGCIL's ₹3.5 lakh crore 5-year plan + DISCOM upgrades + 250 mn smart meters = sustained HV cable demand.
✅Renewables & Solar: Wind + solar plants need massive cabling — intra-plant and grid evacuation. Solar is now Polycab's fastest FMEG category.
✅Data Centres + EV: Data centre boom + EV charging networks = specialised cable demand across all players.
🌍 Export / Global Drivers
✅Global Grid Modernisation: US, Europe, Middle East, Africa investing heavily in grid upgrades — benefits Apar (conductors) and RR Kabel (export cables).
✅China+1 Sourcing: Global buyers diversifying from Chinese cable suppliers — structural tailwind for organised Indian cable makers.
⚠️US Tariff (26%): India-origin cables face 26% tariff in USA — reduces competitiveness vs domestic US supply or other geographies.
⚠️Middle East Conflict: Gulf is a major export market — Strait of Hormuz risk elevated, directly hurting exports in Q4 FY26.

Polycab is India's #1 cable maker with 30–31% market share, yet its Q4 FY26 PAT grew only 7% YoY despite 27% revenue growth. What explains this divergence, and is it a structural problem?
Polycab Q4 FY26-ல் Revenue 27% வளர்ந்தும் PAT வெறும் 7% வளர்ந்தது — இந்த வேறுபாட்டிற்கான காரணம் என்ன? இது structural பிரச்சினையா?
▼
English
The PAT–Revenue gap at Polycab in Q4 FY26 has three compounding causes. First, EBITDA margin compressed to 13.1% vs the FY26 full-year average of 13.9% — this was driven by a higher mix of institutional/EPC business (which carries thinner margins than dealer-channel retail wire business) and softness in Middle East exports, where Polycab earns a premium. Second, FMEG continues to be a margin dilutant — FMEG grew 47% YoY in Q4 but still operates below the 8–10% target margin, dragging the blended rate. Third, the revenue base effect: a 27% revenue surge on copper-pass-through inflates the top line far more than the actual volume or margin-contributing growth. This is NOT a structural problem — it is a quarterly mix shift. The structural proof: FY26 full-year PAT of ₹2,708 Cr grew at a healthy clip from ₹2,210 Cr in FY25, and net cash stands at ₹4,190 Cr. The risk to monitor is FMEG margin trajectory — if it doesn't reach 8% EBITDA by FY28, the valuation premium (42x FY27E) faces compression.

▼
English
RR Kabel's inventory build is a genuine dual-signal that requires careful interpretation. The bull case for the inventory: RR Kabel operates in a copper-intensive business where metal prices rose 25%+ in FY26. Pre-buying copper inventory at lower prices before upward moves is standard operating practice — if timed well, it protects margins in subsequent quarters. Additionally, RR Kabel's Project Rise targets 18% W&C volume CAGR — to support that, channel stocking and dealer pipeline building are necessary. The bear case: inventory days rose from 56 to 64, which in a wire-heavy business means dealers are carrying 8 extra days of stock. In a demand slowdown scenario, this creates a destocking risk where dealers reduce orders, leading to a revenue air pocket. The FMEG segment adds complexity — the goodwill impairment risk flagged by auditors on FMEG acquisitions suggests the intangible inventory (goodwill) is at risk too. Additionally, the former CEO lawsuit adds governance uncertainty. Net assessment: the inventory build is a 60/40 split between strategic and concerning. Investors should watch Q4 FY27 inventory days — if it stays above 60, demand absorption isn't keeping pace with supply push.

▼
English
Stripping out the Goldi Solar gain is the essential first step. Adjusted PAT = ₹734 Cr minus ₹283 Cr (post-tax gain, ~₹211 Cr net) = adjusted PAT of approximately ₹523 Cr — a much more modest outcome. On that basis, Havells' "real" Q4 PAT grew close to flat or marginally positive. More important than PAT is the segment-level EBITDA picture. Cables & Switchgear remain the genuine strengths — cables grew in double digits, switchgear margins held. Lloyd (ACs/TVs) is the value destruction centre — a 19% revenue decline plus slipping into operating loss in a structurally growing AC market is alarming. The ECD (fans, heaters) showed resilience. For valuation purposes: Havells at ~₹84,500 Cr market cap trades at ~50x FY27E P/E. If Lloyd is valued at a discount (given losses) and the core cables+switchgear+ECD business at 35x, the sum-of-parts gives meaningful downside to current prices unless Lloyd recovers sharply. The asymmetric risk: if Lloyd turns around in FY27 (normalisation of summer demand + product refresh), the earnings uplift is massive because the operating leverage works in reverse — a ₹100 Cr operating loss becomes a ₹100 Cr profit swing on modest volume recovery.
