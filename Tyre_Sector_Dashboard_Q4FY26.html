🏭 FY26 Sector Verdict — Strong Year; NR Cloud on FY27
India's tyre sector delivered a robust FY26 driven by three Ds: domestic replacement demand, debt reduction, and double-digit volume growth in TBR and passenger cars. MRF maintained India's best tyre EBITDA margin at ~16.8%. Apollo deleveraged dramatically — Net D/EBITDA from 3.2× (Mar 2020) to 0.4× (Mar 2026). CEAT surged from #5 to #2 in premium passenger cars in just four quarters. JK Tyre posted record revenue ₹16,327 Cr and record EBITDA ₹2,089 Cr. Only Balkrishna Industries disappointed — forex losses, margin compression (−240 bps), and weak European agricultural tyre demand. FY27 headwind: natural rubber +7–8% Q1 FY27, US tariff uncertainty on exports. Replacement market remains the structural profit anchor — contributing 53–75% of revenue and the largest share of profits for all domestic players.

Raw Materials · Natural Rubber · Crude Derivatives · Cost Structure · GST · FY27 Outlook
⚠️ Raw Material Warning — Biggest Sector Risk for FY27 Margins

Natural rubber (NR) prices are expected to rise 7–8% in Q1 FY27 — management guidance from BKT. Companies have already implemented 3–5% tyre price hikes with further increases likely. Crude oil-derived materials (carbon black, synthetic rubber, process oils) remain elevated. FY26 margin expansion for most companies came from falling input costs. FY27 may reverse this partially. BKT is already squeezing at 22.9% EBITDA vs 24.8% a year ago. Companies with highest brand power (MRF, Apollo) can pass through; weaker players risk compression.

🌿
Natural Rubber (NR) — ~30–40% of Tyre Cost
India's largest single cost input. Tyre sector consumes 70–75% of India's total NR demand. Domestic supply (Kerala, Tamil Nadu) insufficient — imports from Thailand, Indonesia, Vietnam. Price swings 20–40% in a year based on monsoon, global farm demand, and crude-derived SBR competition. FY27 alert: BKT guided +7–8% Q1 FY27. Companies responding with 3–5% tyre price hikes. Spread = margin compression of ~100–150 bps for companies unable to fully pass through.
🔺
Carbon Black — ~15–20% of Cost
Crude oil derivative. Gives tyres strength and UV resistance. BKT is building its own carbon black plant — unique backward integration in India. When complete, saves ~₹200–300 Cr/year and reduces import dependency. Other companies source from Phillips Carbon, Himadri, and imports. Crude at $75 = carbon black eases. Crude $100+ = expensive. India imports specialty carbon black grades for performance tyres.
⚗️
Synthetic Rubber & Process Oils — ~20–25%
SBR (styrene-butadiene rubber) is the largest synthetic rubber in tyres. Crude-derived — price tracks petrochemical margins. India imports 40–55% of high-performance synthetic rubbers. When crude rose Q4 FY26, SBR followed with a lag. FY27: if crude stabilises at $75–80, synthetic rubber moderates. Reliance and IOCL are key domestic SBR suppliers. Process oils (aromatic + naphthenic) also crude-derived — used as plasticisers in rubber compounds.
🔗
Steel Cord & Fabric — ~10–15%
Steel wire (drawn from hot-rolled coils) forms the belt structure in radial tyres — critical for TBR and PCR. Steel price sensitive. Polyester/nylon/aramid fabric used for tyre carcass. India imports aramid cords for performance tyres (Kevlar equivalent). JK Tyre and Apollo (TBR-heavy) most exposed to steel cord costs. MRF's scale provides better long-term sourcing contracts. Radialisation trend (bias to radial shift) is increasing steel cord consumption industry-wide.
🏭
GST Cut: 28% → 18% — Industry Demand Conceded
GST on tyres has been reduced from 28% to 18%, conceding the industry's long-standing demand. OEM impact: Auto makers get input tax credit (ITC) — the lower rate cuts their input cost → vehicle price relief → auto volume grows → more new tyres. Apollo (TBR OEM) and CEAT (2W OEM) benefit most. Replacement impact: Consumer now pays 18% instead of 28% — cheaper tyres accelerate the replacement cycle, with an estimated ~5–8% volume uplift. MRF and CEAT (strong replacement) benefit most. Status: Rate cut effective — watch replacement volume pickup in coming quarters.

Risks & FY27 Outlook · Sector · Company · Bull / Base / Bear
⚠️ FY27 Key Risks — NR Cost Spike & Global Uncertainty

India's tyre sector delivered strong FY26. FY27 entry conditions are mixed: NR +7–8% (BKT guidance Q1 FY27), crude derivatives elevated, US tariff uncertainty affecting global exports, European demand softness. Companies with strong brand pricing power (MRF) and structural moats (BKT OHT niche, Apollo Europe) are best positioned. Price hikes of 3–5% have been implemented — the question is whether the spread vs input cost rise is enough.
