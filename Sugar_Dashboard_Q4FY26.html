GOVERNMENT REGULATIONS — CENTRAL & STATE (Sugar · Ethanol · Cane Pricing)
🏛️
Central Government Regulations
Ministry of Consumer Affairs · DFPD · Ministry of Petroleum · Cabinet
CANE PRICE
Fair & Remunerative Price (FRP) — Sugarcane
Set annually by the Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (CCEA) on recommendation of CACP. FRP is the minimum price mills must pay farmers across India. FRP for SS 2025-26: ₹340/qtl (for a recovery of 10.25%). State-level SAP in UP/Maharashtra often exceeds FRP — UP SAP for SS 2025-26 raised to ₹400/qtl (+8% over prior year), causing the single biggest margin squeeze for UP millers in Q4 FY26.
SUGAR MSP
Minimum Selling Price (MSP) of Sugar
Central government fixes the ex-mill MSP to ensure mills can recover cane costs before selling below this floor. Current MSP: ₹31/kg — unchanged since Feb 2019 despite two successive SAP hikes. Industry bodies including Triveni and Dalmia management have publicly demanded a revision; the spread compression is the central financial issue for the sector in FY26.
ETHANOL
Ethanol Blending Programme (EBP) & Procurement Prices
Centre mandated E20 blending (20% ethanol in petrol) as the FY27 target under the National Biofuel Policy 2018. OMC procurement prices fixed by CCEA annually. B-heavy molasses ethanol: ₹60.73/litre; C-heavy: ₹56.58/litre; sugarcane juice / syrup: ₹65.61/litre (ESY 2025-26). Ethanol prices for cane-based feedstocks were left unchanged for ESY 2025-26 despite SAP hikes — the critical industry grievance heading into ESY 2026-27 (announced ~Oct–Nov 2026).
EXPORTS
Sugar Export Policy & Quota Mechanism
Centre controls sugar exports via quota (MEQ — Maximum Export Quota). India suspended open-ended exports in Oct 2023 to protect domestic stocks; export quota remains restricted/nil for SS 2024-25 and SS 2025-26. Centre intervenes bilaterally (e.g., to specific countries) within this framework. Domestic surplus/deficit determines annual quota decisions — currently supportive of domestic price firmness.
STRESSED MILLS
Soft Loan / SEFASU Schemes & Sugar Development Fund (SDF)
Centre periodically extends soft loan schemes for distillery expansion (MSDP) and cane dues clearance (SEFASU). Sugar Development Fund provides concessional loans for modernisation. These are critical for Bajaj Hindusthan (legacy debt) and smaller mills — government backstop that prevents sector-wide failure.
BIOPLASTICS
PLA / Bioplastics Policy — Emerging Regulation
Central government's Single Use Plastics (SUP) ban (eff. Jul 2022) and draft Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) rules for plastics create structural demand for bio-based alternatives like PLA. Balrampur's 80,000 TPA PLA plant directly benefits — no explicit PLA price support yet, but MNRE/MoPNG are evaluating incentive structures. To watch for policy specifics in FY27.
🏗️
State Government Regulations
UP · Maharashtra · Karnataka · Tamil Nadu · Uttarakhand
UP — SAP
Uttar Pradesh State Advised Price (SAP) — Key Cost Driver
UP government announces SAP annually (typically Oct–Nov ahead of crushing season). SS 2025-26 SAP: ₹400/qtl for general variety, up from ₹370/qtl — an 8.1% hike. UP accounts for ~35% of India's sugar production; virtually all UP millers (Balrampur, Triveni, Dalmia, Dwarikesh, Avadh, Uttam, Dhampur, Bajaj) are directly impacted. SAP is above FRP and is legally binding; mills cannot pay below SAP in UP.
UP — POWER
UPERC — Co-generation Tariff & Power Purchase
Uttar Pradesh Electricity Regulatory Commission (UPERC) fixes the tariff for co-generation power supplied by sugar mills to the grid. FY26: UPERC revised power tariffs, adding ₹7.4 Cr to Uttam Sugar's FY26 revenue (including ₹6.7 Cr arrear for FY25). Balrampur, Triveni, Bajaj, and Dalmia all have captive/co-gen plants; tariff revisions materially impact non-sugar revenue.
UP — MOLASSES
UP Molasses Control & Excise Regulation
UP mandates minimum molasses storage norms and levies state excise on molasses/ethanol. Non-compliance attracts penalties — Dwarikesh was directed to pay ₹5 lakh for molasses storage shortfall (Feb 2026). UP excise rules govern potable spirit sales (relevant for Dhampur). State excise policy changes can directly alter distillery economics for UP mills.
MAHARASHTRA
Maharashtra FRP + Revenue Sharing Formula (RSF)
Maharashtra mills follow central FRP plus a state-mandated Revenue Sharing Formula — mills must share 70% of revenue (above FRP equivalent) with farmers. MH also has a separate Fair & Profitable Price (FPP) recommendation. Dalmia (Kolhapur) and Renuka (Pune/Sangli) operate under MH framework; MH cane area is more weather-sensitive and cooperative-dominated than UP.
TNERC
Tamil Nadu Electricity Regulatory Commission — EID Parry / Bannari
TNERC governs co-generation tariff and captive power rules for TN sugar mills. EID Parry (7 mills in TN/AP/Karnataka) and Bannari Amman (TN/Karnataka) are subject to TNERC and KERC frameworks. TN mills run for shorter seasons (Bannari Q4 FY26: 77-day average) due to cane availability — regulatory limits on crushing season also apply.
KARNATAKA
Karnataka — Renuka Sugars & EID Parry Operations
Karnataka sets its own cane price (state-advised, close to FRP). Renuka's Panchaganga and Haldia plants are secured under NCD charge in Karnataka. Karnataka/MH operations are Renuka's core; FY26 cane crush was lower 17% due to poor crop yields (below-normal rainfall in SS 2025-26). KERC governs co-gen tariff.
CROSS-STATE
Cane Area Reservation & Minimum Distance Rules
All sugar-producing states mandate a minimum distance (typically 15 km) between mills for cane procurement zones, enforced by state Cane Commissioners. Mills cannot source cane outside their designated radius without permission. This creates captive cane supply — positive for cost predictability, but exposes mills to hyper-local weather events and SAP revisions in their assigned zone.

SECTOR RISKS & FY27 OUTLOOK
⚠️ Key risks
Cane cost ratchet: UP SAP +8% to ₹400/qtl with no matching ethanol/MSP move — every UP miller's FY27 margin starts in a hole if policy doesn't respond.
Ethanol overcapacity: Triveni management explicitly flags excess national ethanol capacity; with E20 achieved, incremental blending demand slows.
Frozen ethanol prices for cane feedstocks despite two cane price hikes — distillery, the sector's profit engine since 2021, is squeezed at both ends.
Monsoon/weather: Uttam's standalone Q4 and Renuka/Bannari crush were weather-hit; recovery rates remain the biggest single swing factor.
Balance-sheet tail risk: Renuka (negative net worth), Bajaj Hindusthan (legacy OCDs).
Export policy: quota decisions can swing realisations within a season.
🔭 FY27 outlook
Tight closing stocks (~4.6 MT) support firm domestic sugar prices into SS 2026-27.
Industry's two asks: MSP hike (₹31/kg frozen since 2019) and ethanol price revision for cane feedstocks. Either is a sector-wide earnings event.
Diversification winners: Balrampur PLA (Q3 FY27), Dhampur potable spirits, Uttam branded sugar + distillery, EID Parry CPG.
Consolidation likely: stressed assets (Renuka, smaller UP units) are targets for clean balance sheets (Dalmia, Balrampur).
CB stance: trough-year financials, policy-option-rich. Track ethanol pricing announcements (Oct–Nov 2026) as the next sector catalyst.

Why did most sugar companies report lower profits in Q4 FY26?
Q4 FY26-ல் பெரும்பாலான சர்க்கரை நிறுவனங்களின் லாபம் ஏன் குறைந்தது?
▼
Answer
UP raised the cane SAP ~8% to ₹400/qtl while sugar MSP (₹31/kg) and ethanol prices for cane feedstocks stayed frozen. Input cost up, output prices flat — margins compressed across the board. Balrampur's Q4 EBITDA fell 22%, Dalmia's PAT halved, Renuka slid deeper into losses.
உ.பி. அரசு கரும்பு விலையை (SAP) சுமார் 8% உயர்த்தி ₹400/குவிண்டால் ஆக்கியது. ஆனால் சர்க்கரை MSP (₹31/கிலோ) மற்றும் எத்தனால் விலைகள் மாறவில்லை. உள்ளீட்டு செலவு கூடியது, விற்பனை விலை அப்படியே — அதனால் லாப வரம்பு (margin) எல்லா நிறுவனங்களுக்கும் சுருங்கியது. பால்ராம்பூர் Q4 EBITDA 22% வீழ்ந்தது, டால்மியா PAT பாதியானது, ரேணுகா நஷ்டத்தில் மேலும் சரிந்தது.
02
Which companies actually grew profits in FY26?
FY26-ல் எந்த நிறுவனங்கள் உண்மையில் லாப வளர்ச்சி கண்டன?
▼
Answer
Only a few: Uttam Sugar (+17% to ₹101 Cr, distillery production +32%), Triveni (+12% to ₹269 Cr on distillery turnaround), Dwarikesh (+32% to ₹31 Cr from a low base), and Bajaj Hindusthan swung from a ₹780 Cr loss to ₹126 Cr profit. The common thread is distillery/diversified revenue, not sugar.
சில நிறுவனங்கள் மட்டுமே: உத்தம் சுகர் (+17%, டிஸ்டிலரி +32%), திரிவேணி (+12%), துவாரிகேஷ் (+32%), பஜாஜ் ஹிந்துஸ்தான் நஷ்டத்திலிருந்து ₹126 கோடி லாபத்திற்கு. பொதுவான காரணம் — சர்க்கரை அல்ல, எத்தனால்/டிஸ்டிலரி வருவாய்தான்.
03
Is EID Parry's ₹333 Cr Q4 loss a real operational problem?
EID பாரியின் ₹333 கோடி Q4 நஷ்டம் உண்மையான செயல்பாட்டு பிரச்சனையா?
▼
Answer
Mostly no — it's exceptional-led. The company booked ~₹591 Cr of financial-guarantee provisions plus impairments tied to closing the loss-making Parry Sugars Refinery (PSRIPL). Core sugar segment PBIT actually improved YoY (₹78 Cr vs ₹56 Cr). Closing the refinery removes a chronic drag from FY27.
பெரும்பாலும் இல்லை — இது விதிவிலக்கான (exceptional) செலவுகளால். நஷ்டத்தில் இயங்கிய Parry Sugars Refinery மூடலுக்கான ₹591 கோடி ஒதுக்கீடுகள்தான் காரணம். அடிப்படை சர்க்கரை பிரிவின் லாபம் உண்மையில் உயர்ந்தது (₹78 கோடி vs ₹56 கோடி). FY27-லிருந்து இந்த சுமை நீங்கும்.
04
What is Balrampur's PLA project and why does it matter?
பால்ராம்பூரின் PLA திட்டம் என்ன, ஏன் முக்கியம்?
▼
Answer
An 80,000 TPA Poly Lactic Acid (bioplastics) plant — ₹3,080 Cr capex, India's first at industrial scale, commissioning Q3 FY27. It converts sugar into a non-cyclical, higher-value product, structurally changing the earnings mix. Debt is rising (₹903 Cr project borrowings) and a ₹450 Cr equity raise was approved. India's single-use plastics ban creates structural demand tailwind.
80,000 டன் PLA (உயிர் பிளாஸ்டிக்) ஆலை — ₹3,080 கோடி முதலீடு, இந்தியாவின் முதல் தொழில்துறை அளவிலான திட்டம், FY27 Q3-ல் தொடக்கம். சர்க்கரையை சுழற்சி அல்லாத, அதிக மதிப்புள்ள பொருளாக மாற்றும். நிறுவனம் ₹450 கோடி பங்கு வெளியீடும் அனுமதித்துள்ளது. இந்தியாவின் ஒரு முறை பிளாஸ்டிக் தடை இந்த திட்டத்திற்கு கட்டமைப்பு சந்தை உருவாக்குகிறது.
05
What's the difference between FRP and SAP — and why does it matter?
FRP-க்கும் SAP-க்கும் என்ன வித்தியாசம் — ஏன் முக்கியம்?
▼
Answer
FRP (₹340/qtl) is the minimum central government mandated price. SAP is the State Advised Price — UP's is ₹400/qtl, legally binding and higher. The gap (₹60/qtl) is entirely borne by the mill with no relief from the centre. For a mill crushing 1 crore quintals, this ₹60/qtl gap costs ₹600 Cr extra per year. That's why UP millers are the most squeezed in Q4 FY26.
FRP (₹340/குவிண்டால்) என்பது மத்திய அரசு நிர்ணயிக்கும் குறைந்தபட்ச விலை. SAP என்பது மாநில அரசின் விலை — உ.பி.-ல் ₹400/குவிண்டால், சட்டப்பூர்வமாக கட்டாயம். இந்த ₹60/குவிண்டால் வித்தியாசத்தை ஆலை மட்டுமே தாங்க வேண்டும். 1 கோடி குவிண்டால் அரைக்கும் ஆலைக்கு ₹600 கோடி கூடுதல் செலவு — அதனால்தான் Q4 FY26-ல் உ.பி. நிறுவனங்கள் மிகவும் பாதிக்கப்பட்டன.
06
What's the single biggest catalyst to watch for FY27?
FY27-க்கு கவனிக்க வேண்டிய மிக முக்கிய catalyst எது?
▼
Answer
Ethanol price revision for cane-based feedstocks ahead of ESY 2026-27 (announced Oct–Nov 2026), and/or a sugar MSP hike above ₹31/kg. Either one re-prices the entire sector's earnings. Tight closing stocks (~4.6 MT) already support firm sugar prices going into the new season.
ESY 2026-27-க்கான (அக்டோபர்–நவம்பர் 2026 அறிவிப்பு) கரும்பு அடிப்படை எத்தனால் விலை திருத்தம், மற்றும்/அல்லது சர்க்கரை MSP ₹31/கிலோவுக்கு மேல் உயர்வு. இரண்டில் எது நடந்தாலும் முழுத் துறையின் வருவாயும் மறு மதிப்பீடு பெறும். மூடும் இருப்பு (~4.6 மில்லியன் டன்) சர்க்கரை விலையை தக்க வைக்கும்.
