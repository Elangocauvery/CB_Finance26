⚡ The Market Coupling Issue — the single biggest swing factor for IEX

What it is: Today each exchange (IEX, PXIL, HPX) discovers its own price. Under market coupling, a central operator (Grid-India as Market Coupling Operator) pools all buy/sell bids across every exchange and computes one uniform market-clearing price. CERC has approved it, starting with the Day-Ahead Market.

Why it threatens IEX: IEX's edge is its liquidity-driven price discovery — participants come to IEX because that is where the best price forms. If the price is identical everywhere, that advantage collapses (the analogy: forcing every e-commerce site to show the same price erodes the leader's pull). IEX earns ~78-85% of revenue from per-unit transaction fees, so any loss of volume share — or a regulator-led fee "rationalisation" — hits the topline directly. Its DAM share is ~98.8%, and DAM contributes 80%+ of trading volume, so that is exactly the segment most exposed.

Timeline & market reaction
Jul 2025
CERC approves market coupling after 2+ years of deliberation; phased rollout to begin with DAM. IEX stock crashes up to ~28-30% in a day — worst since its 2017 IPO.
Feb 2026
APTEL (Appellate Tribunal for Electricity) dismisses IEX's plea against the earlier CERC directive.
Apr 2026
CERC releases the formal draft notification codifying the framework; Grid-India to frame the Power Market Coupling Procedure within 6 months. IEX falls ~6-8% intraday. Comments invited by 16 May 2026.
Watch
Exact DAM/RTM go-live dates and the transaction-fee design are the key unknowns. Any delay, dilution or rollback reduces pressure; full implementation reshapes the model. Jefferies cut target to ~₹105 citing this risk.

Net: The operations are flawless (record volumes, ~86% margins). The stock is regulation-gated — it trades on the market-coupling outcome, not on the earnings print.

🌍 Could Airtel be India's first trillion-dollar company? Why it's in the conversation

A $1 trillion valuation is ~₹85 lakh crore. Airtel's market cap is ~₹11.14 lakh crore (~$130 bn) — so it would need to roughly 7-8× from here. No Indian company is close yet: Reliance (~$200 bn) leads, followed by HDFC Bank, TCS and Airtel itself among the most-valued. So "first trillion-dollar Indian company" is a long-horizon thesis, not a near-term call — but the reasons Airtel is named as a contender are real:

The bull argument (why it's a candidate):

• ARPU runway — India ARPU at ₹257 is still low globally; structural tariff hikes flow almost entirely to profit.
• Multiple compounding engines — not just telecom: Africa (data > voice now, mobile-money IPO pending), Homes broadband (+37%), Indus Towers (~51%), Payments Bank entering lending, Nxtra data centres scaling to 1 GW, and a sovereign cloud.
• Sum-of-the-parts re-rating — if Airtel Money, Nxtra and the lending business are listed/valued separately, hidden value could surface.
• Operating leverage + deleveraging — 57.8% EBITDA margin and net-debt/EBITDA down to 1.29x means more profit and a stronger balance sheet each year.
• Duopoly economics — India telecom is now a pricing-disciplined market, not a race to the bottom.

The reality check: reaching $1 trillion needs sustained ~20%+ annual compounding for a decade and a stable rupee. Reliance, given its scale and energy/retail/Jio breadth, is the more-cited front-runner. Airtel's case rests on digital-adjacency monetisation actually delivering. This is analysis of the debate, not a price prediction or recommendation.

The single biggest swing factor for each is different — that is the whole point of analysing them separately:
• IEX → regulation (market coupling). Operationally clean, but the multiple hinges on a CERC decision, not on volumes.
• IndiGo → forex + fuel. The reported loss was an accounting/forex event; underlying operation profitable. Rupee and crude direction decide FY27 optics.
• Fractal → client/segment concentration + valuation. NRR and HLS strength are the engine; TMT weakness and a rich P/E are the vulnerabilities.
• Airtel → ARPU trajectory + Africa forex. Premiumisation flywheel intact; tariff timing and Naira are the variables.
IEX — FY27 watch

IndiGo — FY27 watch

Fractal — FY27 watch

Airtel — FY27 watch
Next tariff hike timing and pace of 5G/data monetisation. Homes and enterprise scaling, lending-business launch, and Africa currency trends. Net-debt/EBITDA improvement supports re-rating.
