Endurance Technologies

India's #1 aluminium die-casting auto component company | BSE: 540153 | Promoter Anurang Jain 75% | Div ₹11.50/share | Debt-free with ₹1,807 Cr cash

⚙️ Transmission Products (Clutch assemblies, gearshift forks)~15% India rev · ₹1,635 Cr
🔋 Li-Ion Battery Packs — NEW (Live Jan 2026 · Pune · 35,000 packs/month capacity)Ramp phase · ~₹200 Cr FY26
🌍 European Operations (Germany — Stöferle · Italy · Stellantis / VW / Daimler OEMs)~25% consolidated · ₹3,700 Cr

European OEM customers: Stellantis (Fiat, Jeep, Peugeot), Volkswagen AG, Daimler AG. Stöferle (Germany) acquisition added precision aluminium machining. European revenue grew 32.5% in Euro terms post-Stöferle consolidation in H1 FY26.
Unique Strengths (USP)
  • 🥇
    India's #1 Aluminium Die-Casting Auto Component Maker. Supplies every major 2W OEM — Bajaj Auto (~40% India rev), Hero MotoCorp, Honda MSCI, Royal Enfield, Ather. Top 5 globally in die-cast auto components. 35+ year relationships create deep switching costs.
  • 🔋
    India's Only Full-Stack EV Auto Component Player. Battery packs (Maxwell Energy / Pune facility) + suspension + braking + alloy wheels = highest EV content per vehicle. Target: ₹8,000–10,000 per EV vs ₹3,000 for ICE — 3x revenue per vehicle uplift.
  • 🌍
    Dual-Market Business (India + Europe). European operations (₹3,700 Cr revenue) provide technology access, OEM relationships and revenue diversification unavailable to domestic-only peers.
  • 💰
    Debt-Free with ₹1,807 Cr Cash. ROCE 30.45%. Strong FCF funds the multi-year EV capex cycle (Maharashtra incentive ₹6,060 Cr, new battery facility, AURIC Bidkin plant) without equity dilution or leverage risk.

Risks & Watchpoints

Sector-wide and company-specific risk factors as of May 2026

Sector-Wide Risks
MEDIUM RISK — SECTOR
EV Transition Margin Headwind Phase
All companies are investing in EV capacity before volumes justify fixed costs. This 2-3 year penalty phase keeps margins below historical peaks. Resolution: FY28+ as EV penetration crosses 15% in 2W and 20% in 4W.
MEDIUM RISK — SECTOR
Geopolitical Supply Chain Disruptions
Rare earth magnet shortages, semiconductor tightening, and freight inflation from Middle East tensions affected FY26. Pricol management highlighted these explicitly in earnings calls. Partially hedged through localisation push.
LOW-MED RISK — SECTOR
2W Volume Cyclicality
Four of six companies (Endurance, Lumax, Belrise, Pricol) are predominantly 2W-exposed. Any India 2W slowdown directly impacts revenue. FY27 outlook is positive (rural income, EV launches), limiting near-term risk.
LOW RISK — SECTOR
Currency Risk on Export Revenue
Endurance (25% Europe), Pricol (28% international), Craftsman (12% exports) face INR appreciation vs EUR/USD. Partial natural hedging through import of raw materials (aluminium, copper).
Company-Specific Risks
ENDURANCE — HIGH
EV Battery Ramp-Up Execution Risk
Li-Ion facility at ~20-25% utilisation (35,000 packs/month capacity). Every quarter at sub-50% utilisation dilutes EBITDA by ~40-50 bps. Ramp depends on OEM EV adoption — beyond Endurance's full control.
UNO MINDA — MEDIUM
Premium Valuation at ~55x FY26 PAT
Stock already down 12.6% YTD after valuation de-rating. Any miss on ₹2,500 Cr EV deployment or EBITDA margin improvement (currently sector-lowest at 11.3%) could accelerate de-rating.
LUMAX — MEDIUM
IAC India NCLT Merger Delay Risk
If second NCLT motion faces delays beyond FY27, the ₹40-60 Cr PAT accretion and EV interior positioning get deferred. External dependency — NCLT scheduling is not management-controlled.
BELRISE — HIGH
Core Revenue Growth at Only 8% vs Peers 20-40%
Manufacturing revenue grew only ~5% in 9M FY26. At ₹18,639 Cr market cap this becomes hard to sustain. Aerospace and Badve merger are catalysts but take 12-18 months to contribute.
PRICOL — MEDIUM
₹680-700 Cr FY27 Capex — FCF Pressure
3x normal annual capex to scale EV cluster manufacturing. Will compress FCF meaningfully in FY27 even as PAT grows. Leverage may temporarily increase before free cash normalises in FY28.
CRAFTSMAN — MEDIUM
Acquisition-Driven Leverage Risk
The $1B aluminium target requires 1-2 more acquisitions. Each could spike Net D/EBITDA above the 2.0x management target temporarily. Antique's Hold thesis is built on this acquisition risk.
