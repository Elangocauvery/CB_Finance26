💎 RANK #1 — Vedanta Ltd (VEDL) | CB Score: 50/60 | Grade: A+
Why #1? The residual Vedanta carries the crown jewel — Hindustan Zinc Ltd (HZL), which alone generated ₹22,056 crore EBITDA in FY26, forming ~39% of the entire conglomerate's earnings. With silver prices surging to $84.3/oz in Q4 FY26 (now 45% of Zinc India's profitability), HZL is transitioning into a silver-zinc-lead powerhouse. The ~63.4% HZL stake is worth well over ₹1.5 lakh crore at market prices alone — making VEDL's post-demerger market cap of ~₹1.14 lakh crore look attractively priced. Add Zinc International (Gamsberg Ph-2 coming in Q2 FY27), the copper restart optionality (Tuticorin), and the critical minerals/displays ventures at near-zero valuation — and VEDL offers the most balanced, lowest-risk portfolio among the five. Net debt at entity level is modest (HZL is net cash). CRISIL/ICRA AA rating maintained. Best risk-adjusted return profile.

🔩 RANK #2 — Vedanta Aluminium (VAML) | CB Score: 47/60 | Grade: A+
Why #2? The market has spoken loudly — VAML listed at ₹527, a 337% premium over its allocated price of ₹121. This is the pure-play India aluminium story that global investors have been waiting for. As India's largest aluminium producer (2.46 MT, #1 in India, #3 globally ex-China), VAML generates $2.9 billion in annual EBITDA with the lowest cost of production in 5 years ($1,752/t, targeting $1,650–1,700 in FY27). The LME aluminium price tailwind (+10% YoY), surging EBITDA margin per tonne ($1,512 in Q4 — +72% YoY), Lanjigarh 4 MTPA alumina ramp-up, and BALCO expansion toward 1 MTPA are all structural growth drivers. The only concern is the $3.5B pro forma net debt (ND/EBITDA 1.3x) — manageable but needs monitoring. For investors seeking a high-conviction growth play on India's infrastructure and EV boom, VAML is the most exciting name in this demerger.

⛏️ RANK #5 — Vedanta Iron & Steel (VISL) | CB Score: 34/60 | Grade: B−
Why #5 (Last)? VISL carries the most execution risk and the weakest current fundamentals. The gap between aspiration (15 MTPA target) and reality (1.5 MTPA current capacity) is enormous — a 10x expansion would require tens of thousands of crores of capex over 8–10 years. ESL Steel (the core asset) was an IBC acquisition — it has a troubled history. Goa iron ore mining is perpetually subject to regulatory risk and court orders. The Liberia (Bloom Foundation) assets add international complexity. At a market cap of only ~₹8,235 crore, the market is assigning near-zero value to the 15 MTPA ambition — correctly so at this stage. This is a speculative bet on a very long-term India steel story. Only suitable for high-risk tolerance investors with a 5–7 year horizon willing to wait for capacity execution.

CB Research Summary Verdict

Vedanta's demerger is India's most significant natural resources restructuring — transforming a misunderstood conglomerate into 5 focused businesses. The pre-demerger combined value of ~₹2.72 lakh crore has expanded to ~₹3.6 lakh crore+ at listing — confirming the conglomerate discount thesis. Shareholders who held VEDL on 30 April 2026 received value in all 5 entities.


The dominant play is VEDL (residual) — priced below its HZL stake alone, with silver acting as a hidden earnings accelerator. VAML is the sector darling that attracted premium valuations deservedly — India's aluminium story is real and growing. VOGL is the value hunter's pick — a profitable, cash-generative oil business at throwaway prices. VEDPOWER offers stability over fireworks. VISL is a long-dated option on India's steel ambition — not for the faint-hearted.
