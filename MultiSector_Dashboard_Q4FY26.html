Tata Communications Limited — Global Digital Infrastructure · Tata Group · BSE: 500483
Business Overview

Tata Communications is a Mumbai-headquartered global digital infrastructure and services company, part of the Tata Group. It is one of the world's largest subsea cable network operators, enabling global connectivity for enterprises across 200+ countries.

Core Data Services (87% of revenue): Global connectivity (MPLS, SD-WAN, Cloud Networking), CPaaS (Communication Platform as a Service), IoT, Cyber Security, Cloud, and Media services. Data revenue crossed ₹21,000 Cr mark in FY26, up 9.4% YoY to ₹21,352 Cr.

Digital Portfolio (≥50% of revenue): Products like Commotion (network transformation), Vayu Cloud (multi-cloud), MOVE (IoT), Kaleyra (CPaaS), and enterprise AI platforms. Digital revenue grew 16.7% YoY to ₹10,621 Cr in FY26. Next-gen connectivity the fastest-growing sub-segment at +24% YoY.

Voice & Other: Legacy voice services declining gradually. The company is actively migrating customers to data and digital. Voice contributes ~12% of revenue.

GCC & Enterprise AI: Significant deal wins in FY26 around network transformation, multi-cloud connectivity, and employee interaction capabilities for Global Capability Centres (GCCs) — a key India-anchored growth driver.

Leadership transition: Ganesh Lakshminarayanan appointed MD & CEO-Designate. Focus on digital breakeven acceleration, stronger sales, and better execution.

Netweb Technologies India Limited — AI Infrastructure & HPC · BSE: 543945 · Sanjay Lodha, CMD
Business Overview

Netweb Technologies is India's leading high-end computing solutions (HCS) provider, with fully integrated in-house design and manufacturing capabilities. It serves AI infrastructure, high-performance computing (HPC), private cloud, and data centre markets — primarily for government and large enterprises in India.

AI Systems (43.4% of revenue, ↑ from 15% in FY25): Custom AI servers, GPU clusters, AI-optimised rack systems. FY26 AI segment grew a stunning 459.6% YoY — India AI Mission and sovereign compute projects are the primary drivers. Partnership with Vertiv for advanced AI data centre rack solutions announced in Feb 2026.

High Performance Computing (HPC): 600+ installed HPC systems across defence, research labs, academic institutions. Government's supercomputing mission (NSM) a major demand driver.

Private Cloud & HCI: Hyper-converged infrastructure for enterprise private cloud workloads. Contributes to recurring deployments.

Data Centre Servers: Standard servers and HPS solutions for large-scale workloads — enterprise and PSU segment.

Make in India advantage: 100% in-house design and manufacturing. Aligned with India AI Mission and National Supercomputing Mission — positions Netweb as a sovereign compute champion. Order book ₹4,724 Mn as of March 31, 2026 (confirmed). Extended pipeline ₹44,315 Mn. Debt-free balance sheet. Dividend ₹3/share declared for FY26.

Azad Engineering Limited — Precision Forged & Machined Components · BSE: 544120 · Rakesh Chopdar, Chairman & CEO
Business Overview

Azad Engineering is a Hyderabad-based manufacturer of highly complex precision forged and machined components for mission-critical applications — clean energy (gas turbines), aerospace, defence, and oil & gas. It supplies directly to global OEMs under long-term agreements.

Energy & Oil and Gas (81.5% of revenue): High-precision turbine blades, vanes, casings, and impellers for gas turbines used in power generation and LNG. Customers: GE Vernova (USD 112 Mn + USD 53.5 Mn contracts), Siemens Energy (USD 90 Mn), Baker Hughes/Nuovo Pignone (multi-year contracts), Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (USD 83 Mn). Energy segment grew 34.2% in FY26.

Aerospace & Defence (17.2% of revenue): Safety-critical structural components for aero-engines, including ATGG engine program with MHI (20 engines to be delivered from FY27). Revenue grew 25.4% in FY26. A key long-cycle, import-substitution story under India's Aatmanirbhar Bharat push.

Manufacturing Moat: Four dedicated lean manufacturing facilities commissioned since listing, including two in FY26 and one (Baker Hughes) in April 2026. Each facility is customer-dedicated — reduces execution risk and deepens OEM lock-in.

Export-Led: 81.5% of revenue is exports — direct USD/EUR revenue, natural hedge against INR. Low domestic dependence reduces regulatory/DISCOM risk.

Long-cycle order book: ~₹6,500+ Cr — equivalent to over 10× book-to-bill. Provides revenue visibility through FY28–FY30.

Varun Beverages Limited — India's Largest PepsiCo Franchisee · BSE: 554160 · Ravi Jaipuria, Chairman
Business Overview

Varun Beverages Limited (VBL) is the second-largest PepsiCo franchisee globally and India's largest, headquartered in Gurugram. It manufactures and distributes PepsiCo's carbonated soft drinks, juices, energy drinks, and packaged water across India, Africa, and other international geographies.

India Operations (≈78% of volume): Holds PepsiCo franchise rights across 27 states + 7 UTs covering virtually all of India. Portfolio: Pepsi, 7UP, Mountain Dew, Mirinda, Tropicana, Slice, Sting, Aquafina. India volumes grew 8.3% in FY26. Sting energy drink is a high-realization, fast-growing SKU.

International (Africa-led, ≈22% of volume): Operations in Zimbabwe, Zambia, Morocco, South Africa, Nepal, Sri Lanka, and Mozambique. Africa grew volume 26.3% YoY in FY26 — the highest growth engine. South Africa JV is a near-term consolidation play.

Beverage Mix: CSD (carbonated) dominates. Sting energy drink outperforms category — priced at ₹20/can, mass-premium positioning, rapid rural/tier-2 penetration. Packaged water (Aquafina) growing with distribution strength.

Distribution Moat: 3,000+ direct distributors, 5 mn+ retail touch points, 130+ production lines across 44 plants. Cold-chain infra (coolers) placed with retailers creates strong shelf presence and brand lock-in.

Leadership: Ravi Jaipuria (Chairman) — one of India's most respected FMCG operators. VBL has grown revenue at 25%+ CAGR over 5 years.

TATA COMMUNICATIONS Tata Communications — Global Digital Infrastructure
Strengths
  • World's largest subsea cable network — irreplaceable global infrastructure moat
  • Digital portfolio at 42.8% of FY26 revenue — business model transformation underway
  • Q4 FY26 EBITDA +14.4% YoY — operational engine healthy despite PAT noise
  • FCF ₹1,474 Cr FY26 — 4× FY25 levels; balance sheet strengthening below 2× Net D/EBITDA
  • NPS of 83 globally / 95 India — exceptional customer satisfaction for a global telco
  • GCC (Global Capability Centre) deal wins — sticky, growing enterprise segment
Weaknesses
  • PAT collapsed 74.7% YoY in Q4 FY26 — even adjusting for base effects, profitability is thin
  • FY26 PAT ₹1,044 Cr (−35.8%) — well below the revenue growth rate of 7.3%
  • EBITDA margin 19.6% — far below the long-term management guidance of 23–25%
  • Debt load ₹9,601 Cr — meaningful leverage for a company with thin PAT margins
  • Digital portfolio yet to reach breakeven — new CEO admits this is a near-term priority
  • Revenue growth 7.3% — below expectations even after adjusting for currency effects
Opportunities
  • Enterprise AI infrastructure: Commotion + Vayu Cloud + multi-cloud = AI-era platform play
  • GCC boom in India — Tata Comm uniquely positioned with connectivity + cloud + security
  • Digital breakeven could be a step-change margin catalyst — new CEO's #1 priority
  • Next-gen connectivity growing at 24% — fastest-growing sub-segment to carry overall growth
  • IoT/MOVE platform: 5G + connected devices supercycle globally
  • Debt reduction roadmap: every 0.5× reduction in D/EBITDA frees significant cash for returns
Threats
  • West Asia geopolitical tension — direct impact on demand and cost structures
  • Intense competition from global hyperscalers (AWS, Azure) in cloud connectivity
  • Currency dependence: FY26 EBITDA partly supported by INR depreciation, not pure operations
  • Two major brokerages (ICICI Securities, Nuvama) cut price targets post results
  • Kaleyra/Switch integration — acquired businesses took time to turn EBITDA-positive
  • Regulatory risk in multiple geographies — telecom licensing, data localisation
NETWEB TECHNOLOGIES Netweb Technologies — India's AI & HPC Champion
Strengths
  • Revenue +90% YoY FY26 — fastest growth in Indian listed hardware/HPC universe
  • AI Systems: 459.6% YoY growth and now 43.4% of revenue — dominant structural shift
  • ROCE 37.5%, ROE 32.9% — exceptional capital efficiency for a hardware company
  • Debt-free balance sheet post FY26 — financial flexibility for FY27 expansion
  • In-house design and manufacture: unique in India, aligns with Make in India and sovereign compute
  • Order pipeline ₹44,315 Mn — extraordinary visibility for a ₹2,184 Cr revenue company

Company Overview

Varun Beverages Limited (VBL) is the second-largest PepsiCo franchisee globally and India's largest, with exclusive rights to bottle and distribute PepsiCo products across virtually all of India (27 states + 7 UTs). The company was founded by Ravi Jaipuria and listed on BSE/NSE in 2016.

Core Business: Manufacturing and distributing PepsiCo's portfolio — Pepsi, 7UP, Mountain Dew, Mirinda, Sting, Tropicana, Slice, Aquafina — across India. VBL is the operational engine behind PepsiCo's India presence; PepsiCo handles brand and marketing, VBL handles production, logistics, and last-mile distribution.

Scale: 44 manufacturing plants, 130+ production lines, 3,000+ direct distributors, 5 mn+ retail touchpoints. Cold-chain assets (company-owned coolers) placed at key retail outlets create permanent shelf presence and retailer stickiness.

International: Operations in Zimbabwe, Zambia, Morocco, South Africa, Nepal, Sri Lanka, and Mozambique. Africa revenues growing fastest — 26.3% volume growth in FY26. South Africa franchise consolidation adds large territory under VBL's balance sheet.

Sting Engine: PepsiCo's Sting energy drink, distributed exclusively by VBL in India, is the highest-growth SKU. Mass-premium pricing at ₹20/can makes it accessible to rural and tier-2 markets — a category-creation play inside an existing distribution machine.

📡 Tata Communications — The Transformation in Progress
Tata Communications delivered its highest-ever quarterly revenue of ₹6,554 Cr in Q4 FY26, capping a ₹24,803 Cr FY26 — a 7.3% revenue growth year. The headline looks stable. The sub-headline is more nuanced.

The PAT collapse (−74.7% Q4 YoY) is a distortion, not a deterioration. FY25 Q4 had large exceptional gains from tax reversals in foreign jurisdictions — stripping that out, the operational picture is healthier. EBITDA +14.4% YoY in Q4 and FCF nearly 4× FY25 levels are the metrics that reflect the real engine.

The central challenge remains the gap between aspiration and delivery. Management has guided 23–25% EBITDA margins for years; FY26 delivered 19.4%. The digital portfolio is growing (16.7%), is now >50% of revenue, but has not yet reached breakeven — that is the new CEO's most important assignment.

What validates the long-term thesis: 13 consecutive years as Gartner WAN Services leader; NPS 95 in India; deal wins in GCC/AI infrastructure; subsea cable network nobody can replicate; and a strengthening balance sheet. The question is patience — how many more quarters before digital breakeven translates to PAT recovery?
🖥 Netweb Technologies — India's AI Infrastructure Champion
Netweb's FY26 is a landmark year that will be referenced for years. Revenue +90% to ₹2,184 Cr. AI Systems: +459.6%, now 43.4% of revenue. PAT +80.9%. Debt cleared. ROCE 37.5%. Extended order pipeline ₹44,315 Mn.

This is not normal hardware company performance — this is a business catching the most important infrastructure wave in a generation. India's sovereign AI compute ambitions, the NSM supercomputing mission, and private enterprise AI adoption have all converged to create extraordinary demand, and Netweb is the only scaled Indian player with end-to-end design and manufacturing capability.

The risks are clear and should not be dismissed: the stock trades at 106× PE and 71× EV/EBITDA. At these multiples, the market has priced in perfection — years of 30–40% CAGR with stable margins. Any execution gap, government order delay, or AI hardware competitive disruption can cause sharp de-rating.

The QoQ revenue decline in Q4 (−3.9%) is a normalisation, not a structural break — Q3 FY26 had surged +165% QoQ. The business remains exceptional. The valuation demands exceptional-plus.
🥤 Varun Beverages — The Quiet Compounder at a Discount
Varun Beverages delivered FY26 revenue of ₹21,257 Cr (+20.9%) and PAT of ₹2,153 Cr (+25.1%) — consistent with its multi-year track record of compounding earnings at 20–25%+ annually. Q4 FY26, as always, is the seasonally weak quarter; the operating picture must be read on a full-year basis.

The investment case for VBL rests on three structurally sound pillars. First, India's per-capita beverage consumption remains well below global peers — the runway for volume growth is measured in decades, not years. Second, VBL's exclusive PepsiCo franchise across all of India means it participates in India's consumption story without bearing brand-creation risk. Third, Africa is a hidden growth engine — 26.3% volume growth in FY26 from a geography that many investors have yet to fully price in.

The stock corrected ~25% from its 52-week high of ₹682 to ₹474 — compressing the PE from ~55× historical average to ~40×. This creates an entry opportunity in a quality compounder that has rarely been available below 45× PE. Capex is near its peak cycle (FY26: ₹3,200 Cr); FCF generation should improve materially from FY28.

The watch items are real: PET resin and sugar costs are structural margin headwinds. The franchise dependency on PepsiCo is an irreducible strategic risk. Africa currency (especially Zimbabwe) requires monitoring. But the quality of the business — franchise exclusivity, distribution depth, Sting's momentum — remains undiminished.
⚙ Azad Engineering — Precision, Patience, and Long-Cycle Power
Azad Engineering delivered its highest-ever quarterly and annual performance in FY26. Consolidated revenue ₹603 Cr (+31.8%), PAT ₹133.6 Cr (+54.4%), EBITDA margin 37.4%, Q4 PAT margin 22.8% — highest ever. The numbers are unambiguous.

What makes Azad exceptional is not just the numbers but the structure behind them. OEM qualification barriers, dedicated lean manufacturing cells, 10.8× book-to-bill, and long-cycle contracts with GE Vernova, Siemens Energy, MHI, and Baker Hughes create a business that is fundamentally different from typical capital goods companies. Revenue is not won quarter to quarter — it is earned through years of qualification and then locked in for a decade.

FY26 was deliberately a year of "consolidation and stabilisation" — management's words. The real ramp is FY27 (steady-state) and FY28 (peak utilisation). The Baker Hughes dedicated facility activated in April 2026. MHI ATGG engine deliveries beginning FY27. These are catalysts already in the order book, not aspirations.

The valuation at 121× PE demands a leap of faith — specifically, that the current order book translates to 25–30%+ PAT growth for 3+ consecutive years. Goldman Sachs's maintained Buy at ₹2,460 post Q4 suggests institutional confidence in that pathway remains intact.
