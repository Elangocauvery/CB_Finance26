🏨 FY26 Sector Verdict — Rate-Led Record Year; Occupancy Tested by West Asia Conflict
India's listed hotel majors delivered a record FY26 despite three shocks — the West Asia conflict (hit inbound travel, especially South India), aviation disruptions, and extreme weather. Growth was rate-led: branded-industry ADR rose ~8.6% to ₹8,600 while occupancy improved 100 bps to 64% (CY25), with demand (+9.1%) still outpacing supply (+7.8%). IHCL posted its 16th consecutive record quarter and best-ever PAT of ₹2,084 Cr; ITC Hotels grew PAT (pre-exceptional) 39% with its first-ever dividend; Lemon Tree had the best year in its history; Juniper doubled PAT; Chalet's annuity CRE engine grew 37%. Balance sheets strengthened across the board — IHCL holds ₹4,345 Cr gross cash, Chalet cut net debt to ₹1,900 Cr, Lemon Tree cut borrowings to ₹1,500 Cr at 7.42% cost. The structural engine — weddings, MICE, domestic leisure, spiritual tourism and a 105,000-room branded pipeline only by 2029 — remains intact. FY27 watch-points: occupancy recovery post-conflict, the GST input-tax-credit loss (~2% margin drag per Lemon Tree management), a heavy supply wave of 100+ hotels in 15 months, and rich valuations.

⚠️ FY26 Reality Check — Demand Still Beats Supply, but Q4 Occupancy Was Tested

CY2025 branded-hotel demand grew 9.1% against supply growth of 7.8%; occupancy improved 100 bps to ~64% and ADR rose 8.6% to ~₹8,600 (industry estimates cited by ITC Hotels). But Q4 FY26 brought the West Asia conflict (inbound hit, especially South India), aviation disruptions and extreme weather — Chalet's occupancy fell 7.7 percentage points even as it raised ARR 8%. The sector's FY26 growth was therefore rate-led, not occupancy-led. FY27 hinges on occupancy recovery, absorption of 100+ new hotels opening within 15 months, and the GST input-tax-credit loss (~2% margin drag).

🛏️
The Three Metrics — Occupancy × ARR = RevPAR
Occupancy = rooms sold ÷ rooms available. ARR/ADR = average room rate actually realised. RevPAR = ARR × occupancy — the single best health metric. FY26 snapshot: industry occupancy ~64%, ADR ~₹8,600. Company Q4 ARR ladder: EIH Associated ₹17,696 > Chalet ₹15,456 > Juniper ₹13,457 > Lemon Tree ₹7,457 — positioning (luxury vs mid-market) decides the rate, occupancy decides utilisation. Rate-led growth (FY26 pattern) is higher quality than discount-led occupancy.
📈
Demand vs Supply — The Core Cycle Equation
Hotels are a classic demand-supply cycle business. CY25: demand +9.1% vs supply +7.8% — pricing power stays with hotels. Horwath HTL pegs the branded-room pipeline at ~105,000 rooms by 2029 — large, but spread over 4 years against double-digit demand growth. Watch: 100+ hotel openings due in the 15 months to Q2 FY27 — micro-market oversupply (e.g., select Mumbai/Bengaluru corridors) can dent occupancy locally even when the national equation is healthy.
💍
Weddings, MICE & F&B — The Non-Room Engine
Indian weddings + corporate MICE are structural demand anchors: they fill banquets, F&B and room blocks in lean periods. Juniper's F&B alone is 28% of revenue; its 49,000 sq ft "Grand Showroom" earned ₹28 Cr in year one. ITC's destination restaurants (Bukhara, Avartana) and IHCL's TajSATS catering (₹1,219 Cr FY26) monetise the same kitchen infrastructure. MICE traction is a key reason brokerages expect RevPAR growth to sustain in FY27.
✈️
Aviation & Inbound — FY26's Swing Factor
Hotel demand rides on air connectivity. FY26 saw aviation incidents/disruptions and the West Asia conflict choke inbound travel — South Indian luxury markets felt it most (ITC commentary). Domestic air traffic + new airports (Navi Mumbai, Noida/Jewar) are FY27–28 tailwinds; airport-adjacent assets (Chalet Sahar, Aurika Skycity, Grand Hyatt Mumbai) are direct beneficiaries. Foreign tourist arrivals remain below potential — recovery is upside, not in the base case.
🏭
GST Change — Demand Boost vs ITC Loss
The GST rate cut on hotel rooms lowers the consumer bill — positive for demand, especially mid-market. But hotels lose input tax credit, an estimated ~2% margin hit (Lemon Tree CMD Keswani), visible in LT's −198 bps Q4 margin alongside renovation costs. Net effect: volume-positive, margin-negative in the transition year; high-occupancy operators absorb it best. Wage Code implementation added a second cost layer across the sector (EIH, Juniper, LT all cited it).

Owned vs Managed — The Asset-Light Fee Machine Is the Sector's Margin Driver

Hotel companies earn in two very different ways. Owning a hotel captures the full P&L but ties up ₹1.5–3 Cr of capital per key. Managing someone else's hotel earns fees (% of revenue + share of gross operating profit) at near-100% margin and zero capital:

Fee evidence FY26: IHCL management fees ₹685 Cr (+22%); Lemon Tree fees ₹169.7 Cr (+14%, third-party +23%); ITC Hotels managed pipeline 67 hotels / ~6,700 keys. Asset-light growth is why huge pipelines need little fresh equity.
