Business Analysis — SWOT · Strengths · Weaknesses · Opportunities · Threats · Q4 FY26 lens
BAJAJ AUTO Bajaj Auto Limited
Strengths
  • India's #1 motorcycle exporter — 44% of revenue from exports
  • EBITDA margin 20.8% — second highest in peer group
  • Cash fortress: ₹18,000 Cr+ surplus cash, zero long-term debt
  • KTM rescue package: €800M deployed (loan + bonds) — equity cost only ~€50.65M; distressed entry secured KTM + Husqvarna + GasGas (74.9% Pierer Mobility); FY26 KTM-Triumph ₹5,000 Cr revenue (+40%)
  • Chetak EV 5L units FY26 — first time double-digit EBITDA margin in EV
Weaknesses
  • No meaningful presence in scooters (domestic gap)
  • Domestic market share eroding steadily vs TVS/Hero
  • Over-reliance on Africa/LatAm for exports — EM currency risk
  • Chetak EV still small at 5% of revenue vs TVS iQube
  • Premium motorcycle segment (above ₹3L) underdeveloped
Opportunities
  • CNG motorcycle (Bajaj CNG bike) — first-mover in alternate fuel
  • RE Africa/LatAm market expansion: 35+ countries already present
  • Chetak EV 2.0 + new 125cc category re-entry
  • 3-wheeler EV: large untapped opportunity in last-mile delivery
  • Premiumisation: 200–400cc segment growth structurally rising
Threats
  • EM currency depreciation dents export realisation
  • Geopolitical disruption in key Africa markets (Nigeria, Ethiopia)
  • TVS & Ola Electric eating into domestic commuter share
  • FAME subsidy reduction hurts Chetak EV economics
  • Raw material inflation (aluminium, steel) squeezes margins
TVS MOTOR TVS Motor Company
Strengths
  • EV 2W market leader in India at 26.2% share (March 2026)
  • iQube: fastest scaling premium EV scooter platform
  • Apache brand: strong youth loyalty in 160–310cc sports bikes
  • Established global presence: Indonesia, Brazil, Europe
  • Chennai engineering DNA: R&D depth in EVs and hybrids
Weaknesses
  • PAT margin only 5.5% — lowest among profitable 2W peers
  • P/E at 58–70× — most expensive 2W stock; any disappointment = sharp correction
  • EV contribution 15% of domestic revenue; needs faster scaling
  • Scooter dominance not yet translating to margin expansion
  • Moderate debt on balance sheet unlike Bajaj/Eicher
Opportunities
  • iQube 2.0 + new EV motorcycle — enter Bajaj Chetak territory
  • SE Asia + Africa export expansion: TVS Ronin for markets
  • Hybrid scooters: first-mover opportunity in India's hybrid gap
  • BMW Motorrad partnership: premium co-development leverage
  • Ronin / Zeppelin EV expansion into lifestyle segment
Threats
  • Bajaj Auto (24% EV share) breathing down iQube's neck
  • FAME subsidy phase-out raises EV ASP for consumers
  • Premium segment: Eicher RE taking addressable market share
  • High P/E leaves zero room for earnings misses
  • Ola Electric's potential price-war in the EV scooter space
EICHER MOTORS Eicher Motors — Royal Enfield
Strengths
  • EBITDA margin 25.5% — best-in-class among all 2W peers
  • Royal Enfield brand moat: cult following across 65+ countries
  • Debt-free, ROCE 40%, ROE 31% — exceptional capital efficiency
  • ₹1,88,000 average realisation — 2.5× industry average
  • Himalayan 450 & Guerrilla: global adventure market traction
Weaknesses
  • Single-segment dependence: 72% revenue from Royal Enfield
  • Volume growth cooling: Q2-Q3 sequential decline; Q4 est. ~280K
  • No EV product yet — Flying Flea is FY27; late to EV party
  • VECV (trucks) susceptible to commercial vehicle cycle downturns
  • Middleweight segment (400–650cc) facing KTM/Triumph/Honda competition
Opportunities
  • Flying Flea C6/A6 EV: can RE replicate premium margin in EV era?
  • 125th Anniversary RE models in FY27 = demand catalyst
  • CKD exports: ASEAN, Latin America factory assembly expansion
  • Super Meteor / Bear 650 global launch: Europe + US premium
  • VECV EV trucks: early-mover in commercial EV segment
Threats
  • Triumph Tiger / KTM Duke / Honda CB 350 RS eating middleweight share
  • RE volume deceleration could trigger P/E de-rating
  • Flying Flea execution risk: EV premium pricing may not hold
  • VECV cycle: any capex slowdown crushes CV segment
  • Global premium bike market sensitivity to recession
HERO MOTOCORP Hero MotoCorp Limited
Strengths
  • World's largest 2W manufacturer by volume — 17.14 L units Q4 ✅
  • Splendor: India's single best-selling vehicle for 25+ years
  • Q4 FY26: Revenue ₹12,797 Cr (+29%), PAT ₹1,401 Cr (+30%) ✅
  • Cheapest P/E in sector (16–18×) + highest dividend yield (4.1%)
  • Massive rural distribution: 6,000+ dealerships in Tier-3/4 towns
Weaknesses
  • EBITDA margin 14.5% — lower than Bajaj/Eicher; thin for scale
  • Over-indexed to entry/mass segment; premium gap vs Bajaj/TVS
  • VIDA EV at only 5% of revenue — EV transition behind peers
  • Post Honda split (2011): technology partnership gap took years to recover
  • Global exports at only ~10% — much lower than Bajaj's 44%
Opportunities
  • VIDA V2 EV: +190% YoY growth — strong catch-up momentum
  • Premium push: Xtreme, Karizma XMR, Mavrick 440 — margin expansion
  • Rural electrification: Hero best-positioned for EV in Tier-3/4
  • Export expansion: 52 countries + dedicated export SKUs
  • Tech partnership: reactivating global R&D after Honda exit
Threats
  • Bajaj & TVS both aggressively taking urban commuter share
  • Ola Electric / Ather targeting Hero's core customer base with EVs
  • Any rural slowdown hits Hero disproportionately (68% domestic motorcycles)
  • Margin pressure: commodity cost spikes with low pricing power in mass segment
  • EV disruption risk: if 2W EV penetration accelerates beyond 10%
ATHER ENERGY Ather Energy Limited
Strengths
  • Technologically superior product: AtherStack OS = best EV software in India
  • Q4 FY26: Revenue +74% YoY; loss narrowing fast (₹100 Cr Q4 vs ₹500+ Cr FY25)
  • 6,000+ AtherGrid charging points: infrastructure moat unmatched by peers
  • Gross margin expanded to 25% — structurally improving unit economics
  • Premium brand perception: Ather 450X preferred by tech-savvy buyers
Weaknesses
  • Still loss-making: FY26 net loss ₹517 Cr; cash burn continues
  • Pure EV only — no ICE revenue to cushion EV transition volatility
  • Limited geographic reach vs Hero/TVS/Bajaj's 6,000+ dealer network
  • IPO factory (Maharashtra) ramp-up slower than expected
  • Dependent on subsidy (FAME/state) for competitive pricing
Opportunities
  • EL Platform (FY27): mass-market EV at ₹80K–1.1 L = volume breakeven catalyst
  • Maharashtra Greenfield factory: 1+ million units/year capacity at full ramp
  • AI-powered OTA updates: each update improves product without hardware change
  • B2B fleet EV: large opportunity in delivery, gig economy scooters
  • Export potential: South East Asia EV scooter markets opening up
Threats
  • TVS iQube + Bajaj Chetak aggressive pricing wars in ₹1–1.5 L EV space
  • Ola Electric despite troubles — pricing at ₹80K disrupts Rizta's market
  • FAME III/subsidy structure changes reduce EV competitiveness
  • Cash runway: if EL platform delayed, funding needs may arise
  • Battery technology leap by larger OEMs could commoditise Ather's tech edge

Bajaj Auto — Export & Cash Moat
Unbeatable EM Export Distribution in 70+ Countries

Structural Export Moat
Bajaj Auto — Balance Sheet Fortress
₹18,000 Cr+ Net Surplus Cash — Absorbed €800M KTM Acquisition, Still Flush
Bajaj deployed €800M as a financial rescue package into KTM in 2025 (term loan + bonds to settle creditors), with the actual equity acquisition costing just ~€50.65M (~$58M) — gaining 74.9% control of Pierer Mobility (KTM, Husqvarna, GasGas) at distressed-asset pricing. Despite this deployment, free cash flow of ₹8,000 Cr in FY26 left surplus funds exceeding ₹18,000 Cr — with zero long-term debt on Bajaj’s standalone balance sheet. This allows aggressive capital return (₹150 dividend + ₹5,633 Cr buyback at ₹12,000/share in a single year), global acquisitions, and EV investment — simultaneously. ROCE of 37% is the highest in the sector on this pristine balance sheet.
Capital Efficiency Moat
TVS Motor — EV First-Mover Moat
India's #1 EV 2W — 26.2% Market Share in March 2026

Brand Identity Moat
Eicher — Capital Return Moat
Debt-Free · ROCE 40% · Best Capital Allocator in 2W
Eicher is the only 2W company with zero debt AND ROCE of 40%. This means every rupee reinvested generates outsized returns. The RE business is so capital-efficient that it funds VECV expansion, Flying Flea EV R&D, global launches, and dividends all from internal accruals. No equity dilution needed.
Capital Efficiency Moat
Hero MotoCorp — Distribution Moat
6,000+ Rural Dealerships — Unrivalled Tier-3/4 Penetration
Hero's 6,000+ dealer network in Tier-3/4 India is a competitive moat no other OEM has replicated. Splendor's repeat purchase rate in rural India is near-monopolistic in the 100–125cc segment. This distribution depth also positions Hero as the natural winner when EVs reach rural India in FY28–30.
Distribution Depth Moat
Hero MotoCorp — Volume Scale Moat
World's Largest 2W Manufacturer by Volume
17.14 L units in Q4 FY26 alone. Hero's scale means the lowest per-unit fixed cost absorption in the industry. The Splendor platform has been manufactured for 25+ years with incremental improvements — a textbook scale moat. This makes Hero nearly impossible to undercut on cost in its core segments.
Scale Cost Moat
Ather Energy — Technology & Ecosystem Moat
AtherStack OS + 6,000 Charging Points = EV Ecosystem Lock-in
Ather's moat is not the scooter hardware — it's AtherStack, an operating system that receives OTA updates improving range, performance, and features post-purchase. Combined with 6,000+ AtherGrid charging points, Ather creates genuine customer lock-in. Switching to a TVS iQube means losing your app, your charging network, and your OTA history.
Technology Ecosystem Moat
Ather Energy — Data Moat
Largest EV Scooter Telematics Dataset in India
Ather has been collecting real-world EV scooter data (range, charging patterns, motor stress, battery degradation) since 2018 — far longer than TVS iQube or Bajaj Chetak. This proprietary dataset enables superior battery management algorithms and reliability improvements. In EV, data = durability = brand trust = pricing power.

🥇 Bajaj Auto — Champion of Q4 FY26
Standalone basis · Most complete set of actuals · 6 May 2026
Bajaj Auto delivered the most complete and convincing Q4 FY26 performance among all 5 companies. The evidence is unambiguous: Revenue ₹16,006 Cr (+32%), EBITDA ₹3,323 Cr (+36%), PAT ₹2,746 Cr (+34%) — all three key metrics hit all-time quarterly records simultaneously. This is not a partial beat; it is a full-spectrum performance.

What makes it the champion quarter: All four business segments grew in double digits — domestic motorcycles, e2W (Chetak), 3-wheelers, and exports — simultaneously. This breadth of growth is extremely rare. What is even more striking is the balance sheet story: Bajaj deployed a €800M financial rescue package into KTM in 2025 — comprising a €450M secured loan to KTM creditors, €150M in convertible bonds, and €200M in earlier debt support. The actual equity acquisition cost was just ~€50.65M (~$58M). This was a classic “distressed asset” entry: Bajaj got controlling ownership of KTM, Husqvarna, and GasGas (74.9% of Pierer Mobility) for a fraction of what a healthy acquisition would cost. Yet despite this capital deployment, Bajaj exited FY26 with surplus funds exceeding ₹18,000 Cr on the standalone balance sheet, against virtually zero long-term debt. Free cash flow for FY26 exceeded ₹8,000 Cr (+30% YoY). That is the definition of a cash-compounding machine. The KTM acquisition was not just absorbed — it has already turned into a growth engine: KTM-Triumph delivered ~₹5,000 Cr global revenue (+40% YoY) in its first full year under Bajaj. Chetak EV crossed 5 lakh units in FY26, generating ~₹4,000 Cr in EV revenue and achieving double-digit EBITDA margins for the first time. Bajaj then returned 100% of FY26 PAT to shareholders: ₹150/share dividend + ₹5,633 Cr buyback at ₹12,000/share (16% premium to market). Even after this full payout, ₹18,000 Cr+ surplus remained. Bajaj Auto Credit (financing subsidiary) saw AUM nearly double to ₹18,835 Cr with PAT surging 11× and ROE of 23% — a new compounding engine entirely absent from most peer analyses.
🥈 Runner-Up: Hero MotoCorp
Hero delivered its strongest quarter in years with record volumes (17.14L units), +29% revenue, and +30% PAT. The sequential acceleration across three consecutive quarters is powerful proof of sustained demand. At 16–18× P/E with 4.1% dividend yield and 30% PAT growth — the pure analytical performance-to-valuation ratio makes Hero the most underappreciated Q4 FY26 performer.
🥉 Notable: Ather Energy
On a different dimension — trajectory — Ather is the most compelling Q4 story. Revenue grew 74%, volume grew 76%, and the loss is narrowing quarter after quarter. For a pre-profitability company, this is the most important narrative. Operating leverage is clearly kicking in and the path to breakeven is becoming numerically visible.
